📖 Definition

What is an Adverb?

An adverb is a word that describes or gives more information about a verb, an adjective, or another adverb. Adverbs tell us how, when, where, how often, or how much something happens.

Types of Adverbs
🎭

Adverb of Manner

Describes HOW an action is done
Question: How?
quickly slowly loudly carefully happily
⏰

Adverb of Time

Describes WHEN something happens
Question: When?
today yesterday soon now later
📍

Adverb of Place

Describes WHERE something happens
Question: Where?
here there outside inside nearby
🔁

Adverb of Frequency

Describes HOW OFTEN something happens
Question: How often?
always usually often sometimes never
📊

Adverb of Degree

Describes HOW MUCH or to what extent
Question: How much?
very quite too almost so
✨

Example Sentences
1
She sings beautifully. Manner
👉 How does she sing? — beautifully
2
We have English class today. Time
👉 When do we have English class? — today
3
Please come here. Place
👉 Where should you come? — here
4
I always brush my teeth before bed. Frequency
👉 How often do I brush my teeth? — always
5
The test was very difficult. Degree
👉 How difficult was the test? — very difficult
6
He reads quietly in the library. Manner
👉 How does he read? — quietly
7
My dog is outside. Place
👉 Where is my dog? — outside
8
She sometimes eats pizza for lunch. Frequency
👉 How often does she eat pizza? — sometimes

Study Tips – Remember This!
🔑

Ask the Right Question

Always ask: How? When? Where? How often? How much? — The answer is usually the adverb!

✂️

Look for -ly

Many adverbs end in -ly (quickly, slowly, carefully). But not all! (fast, well, hard are also adverbs)

📌

Position Matters

Adverbs usually come after the verb or at the start/end of the sentence.

🚫

Watch Out!

"fast" is BOTH an adjective AND an adverb. "fastly" does NOT exist! Say "He runs fast" (not fastly).
