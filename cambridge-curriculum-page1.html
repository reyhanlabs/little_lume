About Cambridge Primary English (0058)

Cambridge Primary English Curriculum Framework is an internationally recognized, competency-based curriculum designed for primary learners aged 5-11 years (Stages 1-6). Developed by Cambridge International, it promotes an enquiry-based approach emphasizing confidence, creativity, and intellectual engagement. The curriculum develops learners who communicate effectively, see themselves as readers and writers, and develop speaking/listening skills for diverse contexts.

The Three Core Learning Strands
📖
READING
Sub-strands:
• Recognition (phonics, fluency)
• Interpretation (comprehension)
• Vocabulary (word meanings)

Focus: Decoding text, understanding meaning, analyzing texts
✏️
WRITING
Sub-strands:
• Composition (ideas, structure)
• Transcription (spelling, grammar)
• Handwriting (letter formation)

Focus: Writing for purpose, correct mechanics, creative expression
🗣️
SPEAKING & LISTENING
Sub-strands:
• Speaking (expression, fluency)
• Listening (comprehension, response)
• Interaction (collaboration, turn-taking)

Focus: Oral fluency, active listening, discussion skills

Curriculum Principles & Approach
✓ Integrated Learning
All lessons combine Reading, Writing, and Speaking/Listening skills. Ideally, each lesson has one focus objective from each strand.
✓ Enquiry-Based Learning
Promotes critical thinking, creativity, and intellectual engagement. Learners investigate, analyze, and reason through activities.
✓ Progressive Skillbuilding
Systematic progression from Stage 1 to 6, with clear learning objectives and building on prior knowledge at each stage.
✓ Diverse Texts & Genres
Exposure to fiction, poetry, playscripts, and non-fiction. Recommended genres vary by stage for age-appropriate content.
