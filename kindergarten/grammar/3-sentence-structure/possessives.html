📖 Introduction to Possessives Starter

What are Possessives?

Possessives show that something belongs to someone. They answer the question "Whose is it?" We use possessives when we want to show ownership or belonging.

Two Ways to Show Possession

With 's
Sarah's book - The book belongs to Sarah
With words
My book, your book, his book, her book

Fun Fact! 🌟

In English, when something belongs to one person, we add 's (apostrophe + s) to the person's name!

🎯 Possessive Words Structure

Two Types of Possessives

Possessive adjectives and possessive pronouns show ownership in different ways.

✏️ Using Possessives Practice

How to Use Possessives

Possessive adjectives come BEFORE the noun they describe!

Possessive Adjective + Noun 🎁

The possessive word comes BEFORE the noun.

✓ Correct: my book, your dog
✗ Wrong: book my, dog your

With 's - Possessive Form 🏷️

Add 's to a person's name to show what they own.

Example: Sarah's book = The book belongs to Sarah
Example: Tom's dog = The dog belongs to Tom

Rules for Using 's ✏️

1. One person: Add 's | Sarah's book

2. Multiple people: Add s' only | The girls' toys
