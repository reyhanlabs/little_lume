📖 Countable & Uncountable Nouns Starter

What are Countable and Uncountable?

Countable nouns are things we can COUNT: 1 apple, 2 books, 3 dogs. Uncountable nouns are things we CANNOT count: water, milk, sand, sugar.

The Two Types

Countable
Can be counted - 1, 2, 3... apple, book, dog, person
Uncountable
Cannot be counted - water, milk, sugar, rice, sand

Fun Fact! 🌟

Countable nouns can be SINGULAR (one) or PLURAL (many). Uncountable nouns usually stay the SAME!

🎯 Countable Nouns Structure

Things We Can Count

Countable nouns can be singular or plural. We use "a/an" or numbers with them.

Countable Nouns 📦

Things that can be counted separately.

Singular: a book, a dog, one apple

Plural: two books, three dogs, many apples

Uncountable Nouns 💧

Things that cannot be counted separately.

Same form: water, milk, sugar, sand, rice, cheese

Always singular verb: The milk IS cold. (NOT milk ARE cold)

Countable

  • a book/2 books
  • a dog/3 dogs
  • a pen/many pens

Uncountable

  • water (not waters)
  • milk (not milks)
  • sugar (not sugars)

✏️ Using Countable & Uncountable Practice

How to Use Them Correctly

Different words go with countable and uncountable nouns.

With Countable Nouns 📦

Use: a, an, some, many, a few, a lot of, several

a book | some books | many dogs | a few apples

With Uncountable Nouns 💧

Use: some, much, a little, a lot of

some milk | much water | a little sugar | a lot of sand

WRONG vs RIGHT ❌✓

Wrong: much apples, a little books

Right: many apples, a few books
