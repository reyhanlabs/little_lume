📖 Introduction to Imperatives Starter

What are Imperatives?

Imperatives are commands or instructions. We use them to tell someone to DO something. For example: "Sit down!" "Open your book!" "Be quiet!"

Commands and Instructions

Commands
Stop! Listen! Stand up!
Instructions
Open your book. Write your name. Sit down, please.

Fun Fact! 🌟

In imperatives, we don't say the subject! We just say the action. We understand it means "you"!

🎯 How to Make Imperatives Structure

The Simple Imperative Form

Imperatives are very simple! We just use the base verb without any subject.

BASE VERB
Sit, listen, open, write, run, jump, eat, read

Positive Imperatives 💪

Tell someone to DO something. Just use the verb!

Examples: Sit down! Listen! Open your book! Come here!

Negative Imperatives ❌

Tell someone NOT to do something. Use "Don't" before the verb.

Examples: Don't run! Don't talk! Don't eat here!

Positive Commands

  • Sit down!
  • Listen to me!
  • Open your book!

Negative Commands

  • Don't sit there!
  • Don't listen to him!
  • Don't open that!

✏️ Using Imperatives Practice

When and How to Use Commands

We use imperatives to give orders, instructions, and advice in everyday life.

At School 🏫

Teachers give commands to students:

Open your books. Write your names. Listen carefully. Sit down, please.

At Home 🏠

Parents give instructions:

Eat your vegetables. Brush your teeth. Go to bed. Don't jump on the bed!

In Sports 🏃

Coaches give instructions:

Run faster! Jump higher! Kick the ball! Don't stop!
