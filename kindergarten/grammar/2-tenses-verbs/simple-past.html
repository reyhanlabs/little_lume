📖 Introduction to Simple Past Starter

What is Simple Past Tense?

The simple past tense tells us about actions or events that already happened and are finished. We use it to talk about the past, about things we did yesterday, last week, or long ago.

Two Types of Past Verbs

Past verbs come in two types:

Regular Verbs
Add -ED: played, walked, jumped, danced
Irregular Verbs
Special forms: went, ate, had, saw, was, were

Fun Fact! 🌟

We use simple past to tell stories and talk about memories. When you say "I went to the beach yesterday" or "She ate an apple this morning", you are using simple past!

1. Regular Past Verbs ✏️

Add -ED to the base verb. Most verbs are regular.

Examples: play→played, walk→walked, jump→jumped, dance→danced, watch→watched

2. Irregular Past Verbs ⚡

These verbs change completely. You need to memorize them!

Common ones: go→went, eat→ate, see→saw, have→had, was/were, do→did, get→got

✅ Positive Sentences

  • I played soccer
  • She went to school
  • They had fun

❌ Negative Sentences

  • I didn't play soccer
  • She didn't go to school
  • They didn't have fun

✏️ How to Use Simple Past Practice

When to Use Simple Past

We use simple past for completed actions, past habits, and to tell stories about what happened.

1. Completed Actions ✔️

Actions that are finished.

Examples: I finished my homework. She drank milk. They played in the park.

2. Past Habits 🔄

Things someone regularly did in the past.

Examples: He walked to school every day. I visited my grandma every summer.

3. Stories & Events 📖

To tell stories about what happened.

Examples: We went to the beach. I had a great time. They saw a movie.

4. Time Words with Past 🕐

Words that go with past tense: yesterday, last week, two days ago, this morning, in the afternoon
