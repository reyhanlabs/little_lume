📖 Introduction to Adjectives Starter

What is an Adjective?

An adjective is a word that DESCRIBES or tells us more about a noun. Adjectives answer questions like: What color? What size? What feeling? Adjectives make our descriptions colorful and interesting!

Why Do We Need Adjectives?

Without adjectives, our sentences would be boring! Adjectives help us paint a picture with words. They tell us more information about the nouns in our sentences.

🎨 Colors:
red, blue, green, yellow, orange, purple, pink, brown, black, white
📏 Sizes:
big, small, tiny, huge, tall, short, long, wide, narrow, fat, thin
❤️ Feelings & Qualities:
happy, sad, angry, scared, tired, excited, beautiful, ugly, kind, mean
💭 Textures & Properties:
soft, hard, rough, smooth, wet, dry, hot, cold, sweet, sour

Adjectives vs Nouns! 🎯

❌ Without adjectives: "I have a cat."

✅ With adjectives: "I have a cute, orange cat."

See how adjectives (cute, orange) make the sentence more interesting?

🎯 Adjective Concepts Structure

The Four Main Types of Adjectives

In Grade 1, we learn four main types of adjectives that help us describe everything around us.

COLOR
+
SIZE
+

+

= Complete Describing Words
Every adjective describes a noun in one of these ways.

1. Color Adjectives 🎨

Words that describe what color something is. These help us identify things by their colors.

Examples: red, blue, green, yellow, orange, purple, pink, brown, black, white, gray

2. Size Adjectives 📏

Words that describe how big or small something is. Size adjectives help us understand measurements.

Examples: big, small, tiny, huge, tall, short, long, wide, narrow, thick, thin

3. Feeling/Emotion Adjectives ❤️

Words that describe emotions and moods. These help us understand how people or things feel.

Examples: happy, sad, angry, scared, tired, excited, bored, shy, proud, kind

4. Quality Adjectives ✨

Words that describe how something feels, looks, or tastes. These describe special qualities.

Examples: soft, hard, rough, smooth, wet, dry, hot, cold, sweet, beautiful, ugly

🟢 Correct (Adjectives)

  • A big house
  • The red ball
  • A happy child
  • The soft pillow

🔴 Not Adjectives

  • House (noun)
  • Ball (noun)
  • Child (noun)
  • Pillow (noun)

✏️ How to Use Adjectives Practice

Where Adjectives Go in Sentences

Adjectives usually come BEFORE the noun they describe. They tell us more about the noun that follows them.

Position of Adjectives

Adjective + Noun:
a big dog | the red apple | a happy child
Multiple Adjectives:
a big, red ball | the happy, sweet girl | a tall, green tree
After Articles (a, an, the):
a soft blanket | the beautiful flower | an orange butterfly

Using "is" with Adjectives

We can also use adjectives after verbs like "is" or "are" to describe something:

Examples: The cat is soft. The ball is red. The day is beautiful.

Adjective + Noun Pattern

ARTICLE + ADJECTIVE + NOUN is the basic pattern:

The + big + dog | A + red + apple | An + orange + ball

🔍 Find the Adjectives!

Look at this sentence: "The cute, brown dog runs fast."

Adjectives: cute, brown (fast is an adverb, not adjective)

Not adjectives: the (article), dog (noun), runs (verb)

💬 Practice Dialogue Interactive

Describing Things in Conversations

Here's how we use adjectives when we talk about things. Notice how many describing words are used!

👧 Lisa:
Look at my new dress! It's so pretty!
👦 Mark:
It's beautiful! What bright colors! It's blue and yellow!
👧 Lisa:
Thank you! I also have a soft, white scarf to go with it.
👦 Mark:
You look very nice! That long scarf is perfect!

📢 Counting Adjectives

How many adjectives are in the dialogue?

pretty, beautiful, bright, blue, yellow, soft, white, nice, long, perfect

Answer: 10 adjectives! Can you identify what each one describes?
