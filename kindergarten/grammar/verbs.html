📖 Introduction to Verbs Starter

What is a Verb?

A verb is an ACTION WORD that shows what someone or something is DOING. Verbs are all about movement, action, and doing things! Without verbs, our sentences would have no action!

Why Do We Need Verbs?

Verbs help us describe what's happening right now or what people do. They make our sentences exciting and meaningful! Without verbs, we can only name things, but with verbs, we can tell what they do!

🏃 Movement:
run, walk, jump, skip, hop, climb, swim, fly
🍽️ Daily Activities:
eat, drink, play, read, sleep, sing, dance, laugh
👀 Looking & Noticing:
see, look, watch, hear, listen, feel, smell
💭 Thinking & Feeling:
think, like, love, want, need, wish, hope

🎯 Verb Concepts Structure

The Three Types of Verbs for Grade 1

In Grade 1, we learn three main types of action verbs that help us describe what people, animals, and things do every day.

MOVEMENT
+
DAILY ACTIONS
+

= Complete Verb Groups
Every action verb falls into one of these categories.

1. Movement Verbs 🏃

Verbs about moving from place to place. These verbs show us going, running, jumping, and traveling.

Examples: run, walk, jump, skip, hop, climb, crawl, swim, fly, slide, roll, dance

2. Daily Action Verbs 🍽️

Verbs about everyday activities. These are things we do at home, school, and play.

Examples: eat, drink, play, read, write, sing, draw, paint, build, sleep, wake, sit, stand, talk

3. Feeling & Thinking Verbs 💭

Verbs about emotions and thoughts. These describe what we feel and think.

Examples: like, love, want, need, see, hear, listen, watch, think, feel, laugh, smile, cry

🟢 Correct (Verbs)

  • I run fast.
  • She eats an apple.
  • He plays games.
  • They love music.

🔴 Not Verbs

  • Fast (adjective)
  • Apple (noun)
  • Games (noun)
  • Music (noun)

✏️ How to Use Verbs Practice

Verbs in Sentences

Verbs usually come after the subject (the person or thing doing the action). They tell us WHAT IS HAPPENING in the sentence.

Position of Verbs

Simple Pattern:
Subject + Verb = I run. She plays. The dog eats.
With Objects:
Subject + Verb + Object = I eat an apple. She reads a book.
With Locations:
I play in the park. He runs at school.

Verb Changes with Different People

Some verbs change depending on who is doing the action:

I play | You play | He/She plays (add -s)

Simple Present Tense for Verbs

For Grade 1, we use verbs in the simple present tense. This means the action is happening now or happens regularly.

Examples: I play, you eat, he runs, she sings, the dog barks

💬 Practice Dialogue Interactive

Real Conversations Using Verbs

Here are some conversations where verbs help us understand what people are doing. Can you find all the verbs?

👧 Emma:
What are you doing this afternoon?
👦 Tom:
I want to play football! Do you want to come?
👧 Emma:
Yes! I love to play football! Let's run and kick the ball!
👦 Tom:
Great! After we play, we can eat ice cream.
👧 Emma:
Perfect! I like chocolate ice cream!
