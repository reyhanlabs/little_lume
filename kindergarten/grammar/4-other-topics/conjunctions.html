📖 Introduction to Conjunctions Starter

What Are Conjunctions?

Conjunctions are linking words that connect words, phrases, and ideas. They help us make our sentences longer and more interesting by joining different parts together!

The Four Main Conjunctions

The most common conjunctions for Grade 1 are:

AND
Connects things that go together
BUT
Shows contrast or change of idea
OR
Shows choices or alternatives
BECAUSE
Explains reasons or causes

Fun Fact! 🌟

Conjunctions are small words, but they are very powerful! They help us express more complex ideas and feelings in our sentences!

🎯 Conjunctions Pattern Structure

How Conjunctions Work

Conjunctions sit in the middle of two ideas and connect them together. Each conjunction has a special meaning and purpose!

The Four Main Conjunctions

AND - Adds similar ideas together
BUT - Shows contrast or surprise
OR - Gives choices
BECAUSE - Explains reasons

Using Conjunctions

Conjunctions connect ideas in many different ways. Learning when to use each one helps us communicate better!

✏️ Using Conjunctions Practice

AND - Connecting Similar Ideas

Use "and" to add information:

Connecting nouns:
I like apples and oranges.
Connecting sentences:
I went to school and I played outside.
Connecting people:
Tom and Emma are friends.

BUT - Showing Contrast

Use "but" to show different ideas:

Different ideas:
The sun is hot but the ice is cold.
Unexpected results:
He studied hard but he got a bad grade.
Preferences:
I like vegetables but my brother doesn't.

OR - Showing Choices

Use "or" to give options:

Asking for choice:
Do you want red or blue?
Offering options:
You can play outside or stay inside.
Preferences:
Would you like tea or coffee?

BECAUSE - Explaining Reasons

Use "because" to explain why:

Giving reasons:
I am happy because it is my birthday.
Explaining causes:
We stay inside because it is raining.
Clarifying:
The bird can fly because it has wings.

💡 Real Examples Stories

Example 1: AND (Adding Information)

Sentence:
I like pizza and ice cream.
What it shows:
Two things I like are connected together.

Example 2: BUT (Showing Contrast)

Sentence:
I like to play but I also like to read.
What it shows:
Two different but related interests.

Example 3: OR (Offering Choices)

Sentence:
We can go to the zoo or the museum.
What it shows:
Two possible choices.

Example 4: BECAUSE (Explaining Reasons)

Sentence:
I am sleepy because I went to bed late.
What it shows:
The reason why I am sleepy.

Example 5: Multiple Conjunctions

Sentence:
I like apples and oranges, but I don't like bananas.
What it shows:
Multiple ideas connected together.

Example 6: AND + BECAUSE

Sentence:
I am happy and excited because I am going to the party.
What it shows:
Two feelings connected by AND, with a reason connected by BECAUSE.

💬 Practice Dialogue Speaking

Mom:
What do you want for lunch?
Child:
I want chicken and rice, please.
Mom:
Do you want milk or juice?
Child:
I want juice, but can you make it cold?
Mom:
Of course! Can you set the table?
Child:
I would like to, but I have homework first.
Mom:
Okay. Do your homework, or we can do it after lunch.
Child:
I am excited because we are going to eat together!
Mom:
I am too! I love spending time with you.
Child:
And you are a great cook! But let me help you next time.
