📖 Introduction to Possessives Starter

What Are Possessives?

Possessives are words that show that something belongs to someone or is connected to someone. They help us talk about ownership and relationships with people and things!

The Seven Main Possessives

These words show who owns or is related to something:

MY
Things that belong to me
YOUR
Things that belong to you
HIS
Things that belong to a boy or man
HER
Things that belong to a girl or woman
ITS
Things that an animal or object has
OUR
Things that belong to our group
THEIR
Things that belong to them

Fun Fact! 🌟

Possessives come before the noun! We never add 's or apostrophes to possessives. They are special words that always look the same!

🎯 Possessives Pattern Structure

How Possessives Work

Every person has a matching possessive word. Understanding this pattern helps us speak and write better English!

Possessive + Noun
Example: My book, Her toy, Their house

Possessives Match With Pronouns

Each pronoun (I, you, he, she, it, we, they) has a matching possessive (my, your, his, her, its, our, their). Learning this connection helps you remember all the possessives!

✏️ Using Possessives Practice

MY - Things That Belong to Me

Use "my" when you are talking about yourself:

Example 1:
My name is Tom.
Example 2:
My dog is brown.
Example 3:
My favorite color is blue.

YOUR - Things That Belong to You

Use "your" when you are talking to another person:

Example 1:
What is your name?
Example 2:
I like your shirt.
Example 3:
Where is your backpack?

HIS - Things That Belong to Him

Use "his" when talking about a boy or man:

Example 1:
His name is Tom.
Example 2:
His toy is big.
Example 3:
I like his idea.

HER - Things That Belong to Her

Use "her" when talking about a girl or woman:

Example 1:
Her name is Emma.
Example 2:
Her book is interesting.
Example 3:
I like her dress.

ITS - Things an Animal or Object Has

Use "its" when talking about an animal or thing:

Example 1:
The dog has its toy.
Example 2:
The tree and its leaves are green.
Example 3:
The car has its color.

OUR - Things That Belong to Our Group

Use "our" when talking about a group that includes you:

Example 1:
Our class is fun.
Example 2:
Our school is big.
Example 3:
Our teacher is nice.

THEIR - Things That Belong to Them

Use "their" when talking about other people:

Example 1:
Their house is pretty.
Example 2:
Their names are Tom and Emma.
Example 3:
Their toys are in the box.

💡 Real Examples Stories

Example 1: MY

Sentence:
My mother makes good cookies.
What it shows:
The cookies belong to my mother.

Example 2: YOUR

Sentence:
Your pencil is red.
What it shows:
The red pencil belongs to you.

Example 3: HIS

Sentence:
His sister is in Grade 2.
What it shows:
The sister belongs to him.

Example 4: HER

Sentence:
Her apple is on the table.
What it shows:
The apple belongs to her.

Example 5: ITS

Sentence:
The bird has its nest in the tree.
What it shows:
The nest belongs to the bird.

Example 6: OUR & THEIR

Sentence:
Our classroom is next to their classroom.
What it shows:
One classroom belongs to us, the other to them.
