🎯 Learning Goals

Greet Friends

🙋

Talk About Me

Use simple questions like "What is this?" and "Can I…?"

📚 Lessons

5 Fun Speaking Lessons

Each lesson is 20–25 minutes of play-based speaking practice.

1

Hello, World! 🌍

Greetings & Introductions

Children learn to greet others, say their name, and respond to simple questions. Repetition through songs and role-play builds confidence right away.

Hello! / Hi!Good morning! My name is ___.Nice to meet you! Goodbye! / Bye-bye!
🎵 Activity: "Hello Song" Circle Game Children sit in a circle. The teacher holds a puppet and says "Hello! My name is Bunny." Each child greets the puppet and introduces themselves in turn.
2

All About Me! 🧒

Personal Information

Young learners practice speaking about themselves — their age, their family, and what they love. Short sentence frames make it easy and fun.

I am ___ years old.I like ___. I have a ___.My favourite colour is ___. I am happy / sad / hungry.
🖍️ Activity: "Me Bag" Show & Tell Each child brings a small bag with three favourite items. They stand up and say one sentence about each item: "This is my teddy bear. I like my teddy bear!"
3

Colours & Animals 🦁🌈

Vocabulary in Context

Children learn to name colours and animals through chants and picture cards, then practice putting words into short sentences for real communication.

The cat is orange.I see a dog! What colour is it?It is a big elephant. The frog is green.
🃏 Activity: Flashcard Races Teacher holds up a flashcard. The first child to shout the correct sentence — e.g. "It is a yellow duck!" — wins a sticker. Fast, loud, and full of laughter!
4

Yummy Food! 🍎🍕

Food & Preferences

Talking about food is naturally motivating for young children. Learners practice expressing likes and dislikes, making requests, and answering questions at a pretend café.

I like apples!I don't like broccoli. Can I have some ___?Yes, please! / No, thank you. It is yummy / yucky!
☕ Activity: BunRey Café Role-Play Set up a pretend café with toy food or picture menus. One child is the waiter, one is the customer. They practice ordering, serving, and saying thank you.
5

Where Is It? 📦

Simple Questions & Locations

Children learn to ask and answer questions about location using simple prepositions. A hide-and-seek game keeps every child engaged and speaking.

Where is the ___?It is on / in / under the ___. Look! Over there!I found it! Is it here? Yes / No!
🔍 Activity: Classroom Treasure Hunt The teacher hides a stuffed animal around the room. Children ask "Is it under the table?" or "Is it on the chair?" and the teacher answers until they find it!

💡 Tips

Tips for Teachers & Parents

Help your little one shine brighter every day!

🎵

Sing Every Day

Songs wire new words into long-term memory. Sing target phrases in the car, at meals, and at bedtime — repetition is the magic.

🎭

Role-Play Together

Pretend to be characters from stories. Children speak freely when there's no pressure — only play. Even 5 minutes counts!

🏆

Celebrate Every Word

Clap and cheer for every attempt, even imperfect ones. Confidence at age 5 is the foundation of fluency at age 15.

👂

Listen More, Correct Less

Let children finish their sentences before gently modelling the correct form. Interrupting to correct stops speaking before it starts.

📺

English Media for 10 min

Short English cartoons or nursery rhyme videos (10–15 min/day) expose children to natural rhythm and pronunciation effortlessly.

🌟

Keep It Positive

End every session with a high five and one phrase the child got right. Positive closure builds the habit of coming back for more.
