🌈 Grammar Focus

Simple patterns, explained with color

"This is my..." (one thing)

This is my nose.

"I have two..." (more than one)

I have two hands.

Singular vs Plural

one nose, one mouth
→ two eyes, two ears, ten fingers

Possessive: my

"My" shows it belongs to me. "This is my head."

🧑‍🏫 Teacher Notes

Preparation

Review vocabulary flow, test audio/speaker volume, prepare open floor space for TPR games.

Materials

Device/projector, speaker, optional printed flashcards, playdough, paper plates for sensory activity.

Classroom Management

Keep TPR games under 3 minutes each. Use a calm-down chant to transition back to sitting.

Differentiation

Fast finishers: let them lead Simon Says or Show & Tell. Struggling learners: pair with a buddy, allow pointing before speaking.

Home Practice

Ask families to play "Touch your..." at home during bath time or getting dressed.
