Simple Past Tense

🎮 Discover what happened in the past! Learn about yesterday's adventures! 🎮
🎯 What is Simple Past Tense?
We use Simple Past Tense to talk about actions that happened in the past and are now finished! It's like telling stories about what you did yesterday, last week, or a long time ago!
✅ What You Did
I played soccer yesterday.
✅ Actions in the Past
She ate lunch at noon.
✅ Completed Events
They went to the park.
⏰ When Do We Use Simple Past?

1. Actions That Finished in the Past

Things that happened and are completely done now.

I watched a movie last night.

2. Completed Events or Routines

Regular things you did in the past that you don't do anymore.

She studied hard for the exam last week.

3. Telling Stories

When you tell someone what happened in the past.

The children played in the garden yesterday.

4. Specific Time in the Past

When you mention when something happened (yesterday, last week, etc).

I ate breakfast two hours ago.
⭐ Regular Verbs: Add -ed!
For most verbs, we add -ed to make the past tense! It's like adding a special ending to show that it happened in the past!
play → played
I played soccer. ⚽
watch → watched
She watched TV. 📺
walk → walked
They walked home. 🚶
study → studied
He studied math. 📚
dance → danced
She danced at the party. 💃
jump → jumped
They jumped high. 🤸
🎲 Irregular Verbs: Special Friends!
Some verbs are SPECIAL! They don't follow the regular pattern. They have their own special past tense forms! These are called Irregular Verbs! 🌟
go → went
I went to school.
eat → ate
She ate lunch.
see → saw
They saw a movie.
have → had
He had fun.
get → got
I got a new toy.
do → did
They did homework.
come → came
She came to school.
make → made
We made a cake.
sit → sat
I sat down.
write → wrote
She wrote a letter.
🌟 Was and Were (Past of "To Be")

Using WAS (for I, He, She, It)

Use "was" for one person or thing in the past.

I was happy yesterday.
She was a good student.
He was at school.

Using WERE (for You, We, They)

Use "were" for more than one person or thing in the past.

We were excited at the party.
They were playing outside.
You were very funny!
📝 Three Forms of Simple Past
✅ POSITIVE

Saying what happened

I played soccer.
She ate lunch.
They went home.
❌ NEGATIVE

Saying what did NOT happen

I did not play soccer.
She did not eat lunch.
They did not go home.
❓ QUESTION

Asking about the past

Did you play soccer?
Did she eat lunch?
Did they go home?
🎯 Using Did and Didn't

For NEGATIVE Sentences: Did NOT (Didn't)

Use "did not" or "didn't" + the base verb (without -ed)!

I did not play soccer. (NOT: did not played)
She didn't eat lunch. (NOT: didn't ate)

For YES/NO QUESTIONS: Did

Use "did" + subject + base verb!

Did you play soccer? (NOT: Did you played?)
Did she eat lunch?

🔑 Important Rule!

When using "did," the main verb stays in its base form (without -ed)!

✅ She did NOT play. (correct)
❌ She did NOT played. (wrong!)
