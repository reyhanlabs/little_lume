Simple Present Tense

Learn how to talk about habits and facts!
🎯 What is Simple Present Tense?
Simple Present Tense is used to talk about things that happen every day, things that are always true, or habits. We use it when something is not happening right now, but happens regularly or is a general fact!
Examples:
• I eat breakfast every morning.
• My sister plays soccer on weekends.
• Birds fly in the sky.
• The sun rises in the east.
⏰ When Do We Use Simple Present?

1. Daily Habits and Routines

Things you do every day or regularly

I brush my teeth every morning.
Translation: Saya menyikat gigi setiap pagi.

2. Facts That Are Always True

General truths about the world

Water boils at 100 degrees.
Translation: Air mendidih pada 100 derajat.

3. Things You Like or Dislike

Your personal preferences and feelings

I love chocolate ice cream!
Translation: Aku menyukai es krim cokelat!

4. Regular Schedule or Timetable

When something happens at a specific time regularly

The bus arrives at 8 o'clock.
Translation: Bus tiba pada jam 8.

📐 Simple Present Formula
For Most Subjects (I, You, We, They)
Subject + Verb + Rest of Sentence
Example: I play soccer.
Example: They eat rice every day.
For He, She, It (Add -s or -es)
Subject + Verb + -s/-es + Rest of Sentence
Example: He plays soccer. (play → plays)
Example: She watches TV. (watch → watches)
✅ Important Grammar Rules

Rule 1: Adding -s to Verbs

When the subject is he, she, or it, we add -s to the verb:

He plays guitar.
She walks home.
It runs fast.

Rule 2: Adding -es to Verbs Ending in s, x, z, ch, sh

If the verb ends with these letters, we add -es:

watch → He watches movies. (ends with -ch)
kiss → She kisses her mom. (ends with -s)
wash → She washes her hands. (ends with -sh)

Rule 3: Changing -y to -ies

If the verb ends with a consonant + -y, change it to -ies:

study → He studies math.
cry → The baby cries.
carry → She carries her bag.

Rule 4: Do NOT Change for I, You, We, They

Only add -s/-es for he, she, it. Others stay the same:

I play soccer. (NOT: I plays)
You run fast. (NOT: You runs)
We study together. (NOT: We studies)
They eat lunch. (NOT: They eats)
📝 Three Forms of Simple Present
✅ POSITIVE (Affirmative)

Saying what IS true

I play basketball.
He watches movies.
They eat apples.
❌ NEGATIVE (Denying)

Saying what is NOT true

I do not (don't) play basketball.
He does not (doesn't) watch movies.
They do not (don't) eat apples.
❓ QUESTION (Asking)

Asking about something

Do you play basketball?
Does he watch movies?
Do they eat apples?
🚫 Making Negative Sentences
Negative Formula (With do/does)
Subject + do not (don't) + Verb + Rest
Example: I don't play soccer.

For I, You, We, They: Use "don't"

I don't like broccoli.
You don't study on Sundays.
We don't eat meat.
They don't watch horror movies.

For He, She, It: Use "doesn't"

⚠️ Important: When using "doesn't," the main verb goes back to its base form (without -s/-es)!

He doesn't like broccoli. (NOT: doesn't likes)
She doesn't study at night.
It doesn't work properly.
❓ Making Questions
Question Formula (Yes/No Questions)
Do/Does + Subject + Verb + Rest?
Example: Do you play soccer?
Example: Does she like pizza?

For I, You, We, They: Use "Do"

Do I need a pencil?
Do you like ice cream?
Do we have homework?
Do they play games?

For He, She, It: Use "Does"

⚠️ Important: The main verb returns to base form when using "does"!

Does he like chocolate? (NOT: Does he likes)
Does she play tennis?
Does it work?
💡 Tip: When you answer "yes" to a question with "does," the answer must include "does," and the verb goes back to its simple form: "Does he play soccer?" "Yes, he does." (NOT: Yes, he does plays)

⭐ Key Points to Remember

✨ Most Important Rules

1. Simple Present is for habits, facts, and daily routines.

2. Add -s/-es to the verb only for he, she, and it.

3. Use don't for I, you, we, they → Use doesn't for he, she, it.

4. Use do for I, you, we, they → Use does for he, she, it in questions.

5. After "doesn't" or "does," always use the base form of the verb (no -s/-es added).
