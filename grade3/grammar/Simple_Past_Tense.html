The Magic Rules

How do we make a verb travel to the past?

🟢 Regular Verbs EASY

Just add -ed to the end of the verb!

walk ➜ walked
  • play ➜ played
  • jump ➜ jumped
  • cook ➜ cooked
  • If the verb ends with e, just add -d: like ➜ liked
  • If it ends with a consonant + y, change y ➜ i + ed: cry ➜ cried

🩷 Irregular Verbs TRICKY

These verbs do NOT follow the -ed rule. They change into a whole new word!

go ➜ went
  • go ➜ went
  • eat ➜ ate
  • see ➜ saw
  • You just have to remember them — like magic spells! 🪄

Past Tense in Action

See it in real sentences!

🐶
My dog barked at the mailman this morning.
🏖️
We visited the beach last summer.
📚
I read two books last night.
🎂
She baked a chocolate cake yesterday.
😢
It did not rain last week. (negative form!)
❓
Did you brush your teeth this morning? (question form!)

➕ Negative Sentences

did not (didn't) + verb

I didn't play outside. (verb goes back to its base form!)

❓ Question Sentences

Did + subject + verb?

Did you eat breakfast?
