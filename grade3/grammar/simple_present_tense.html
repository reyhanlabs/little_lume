📖 Let's Learn

What is the Simple Present Tense?

We use the Simple Present Tense for three big reasons:

1️⃣ Daily habits & routines I brush my teeth every morning.
2️⃣ Facts that are always true The sun rises in the east.
3️⃣ Likes, dislikes & feelings She loves ice cream.

🐢 Think of it like a turtle's favorite path — it walks it again and again, every single day. That's what Simple Present is all about: things that happen regularly or are always true!

🧩 The Magic Formula

How Do We Build a Sentence?

Subject + Verb (base form / +s / +es) + Object
I / You / We / They + Verb  |   He / She / It + Verb + s/es

Here is the most important rule of Simple Present — remember Lumi's golden rule:

When the subject is He, She, or It (just ONE person, animal, or thing), the verb wears a little hat: +s or +es! 🎩
I play football. no extra letter
You play football. no extra letter
We play football. no extra letter
They play football. no extra letter
He plays football. add +s 🎩
She plays football. add +s 🎩
It plays in the yard. add +s 🎩
The cat runs fast. add +s 🎩
🔤 Spelling Rules

How Do We Spell the +s / +es Verbs?

RuleExample
most verbs add +swalk → walks, eat → eats, run → runs
verb ends in s, x, ch, sh, o → add +eswatch → watches, fix → fixes, go → goes, wash → washes
verb ends in consonant + y → change y to i + esstudy → studies, fly → flies, cry → cries
verb ends in vowel + y → just add +splay → plays, say → says
special verb have → becomeshave → has (He has a dog 🐶)

🌟 Don't worry if this feels tricky at first — practice makes it stick like glue! We'll play a sorting game below to help you remember.

🚫 Yes & No

Saying "No" and Asking Questions

✅ Positive (true)

  • I like mangoes.
  • She plays the piano.

❌ Negative (not true)

  • I / You / We / They + don't + verb
  • He / She / It + doesn't + verb
  • I don't like spinach.
  • He doesn't play the piano.
Do/Does + Subject + Verb (base form) + ... ?
Do you like pizza?  |  Does she like pizza?

💡 Remember: when we use doesn't or does, the main verb goes back to its plain form — NO +s!
Example: He doesn't play (not "plays") and Does he play? (not "plays?")

⏰ Time Word Friends

Words That Love the Simple Present

These special "time word friends" often appear in Simple Present sentences. Spot them and you'll know which tense to use!

always I always brush my teeth.
usually She usually walks to school.
often We often play outside.
sometimes He sometimes reads comics.
never They never tell lies.
every day/week I eat breakfast every day.
