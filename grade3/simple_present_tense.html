Understanding Simple Present Tense

Master the rules and patterns of simple present tense

What is Simple Present?

Simple Present Tense is used to describe actions that happen regularly, facts, or general truths. It's the most common tense in English.

"I play football every day." / "She works in a bank."

Subject Pronoun Rules

He/She/It: Add -s or -es to the verb
I/You/We/They: Use the base form of the verb

He plays / She watches / They play

Verb Patterns

Affirmative: Subject + Verb + Object
Negative: Subject + do/does + not + Verb
Question: Do/Does + Subject + Verb?

"Does he like ice cream?" / "I don't like spicy food."

Adding -s or -es

-s: Most verbs (play → plays)
-es: Verbs ending in x, z, ch, sh, s (watch → watches)

fix → fixes, buzz → buzzes, push → pushes

When to Use It

Use Simple Present for: habitual actions, daily routines, permanent facts, general truths, scheduled events

"The sun rises in the east." / "She exercises three times a week."

Special Cases

Verbs ending in y: Change y to ies (study → studies)
Irregular: has, does, goes

cry → cries, fly → flies, carry → carries
