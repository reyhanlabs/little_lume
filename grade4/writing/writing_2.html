📌 Lesson 1 – Sentence Structure
📝 What Is a Sentence?
A sentence in English must have a Subject (S) and a Verb (V). It starts with a capital letter and ends with a full stop (.), question mark (?), or exclamation mark (!).
Subject
The person, animal, or thing that does the action.
Examples: I, She, My cat, The students
Verb
The action word — what the subject does.
Examples: eat, play, is, go, write
Object
The thing that receives the action (optional).
Examples: rice, a book, football
✨ Example Sentences
  • I eat rice every morning.
  • She reads a book in the library.
  • My cat plays with a ball.
  • The students write English sentences.

  5. [ in the garden / beautiful flowers / There are ] →
📌 Lesson 2 – Descriptive Text
🔍 Describing Something
A descriptive text describes a person, animal, object, or place in detail. We use adjectives (describing words) to make our writing more vivid and interesting.
Identification
Introduce the subject you are describing.
"This is my pet. Its name is Milo."
Description
Describe its features: colour, size, character, shape, etc.
"Milo is small and fluffy. He has orange fur and green eyes."
📄 Sample Text – My Pet

This is my pet cat. His name is Milo. He is small and fluffy. He has soft orange fur and bright green eyes. Milo is very friendly and playful. He loves to sleep on my bed. I always give him milk in the morning. Milo is my best friend at home.

📌 Lesson 3 – Recount Text
🕐 Retelling an Experience
A recount text retells a past experience or event in the order it happened. We use the Simple Past Tense: verbs change to their past form.
Examples: go → went · eat → ate · see → saw · play → played
Orientation
Who? Where? When? Sets the scene.
"Last Sunday, I went to the zoo with my family."
Events
What happened, in order. Use time connectives.
First… Then… After that… Finally…
Re-orientation
Closing — your feelings or a summary.
"It was a wonderful day. I was very happy."
📄 Sample Text – My Trip to the Zoo

Last Sunday, I went to the zoo with my family. We left home at eight o'clock in the morning.

First, we visited the elephant enclosure. The elephants were very big and funny. Then, we saw colourful birds and playful monkeys. I ate ice cream near the lake. After that, my little sister fed the rabbits. She was very excited.

Finally, we went home at four o'clock. It was a wonderful day. I felt very happy and tired.

📌 Lesson 4 – Short Message & Greeting Card
💌 Writing a Short Message
A short message is a brief piece of writing to share information or express a feeling. It has three parts: To (receiver), Message (content), and From (sender).
💌 Sample Short Message

To: Risa
Hi Risa! I cannot come to school today because I am sick. Please tell our teacher. I will be back tomorrow. See you!
From: Andi

🎉 Sample Greeting Card – Birthday

To: Dina
Happy Birthday, Dina! 🎂
I hope you have a wonderful day. May all your wishes come true. You are the best friend ever! Let's celebrate together!
With love, Sari

💡 Tips for Good Writing
🔡
Capital Letters Always start a sentence with a capital letter. Names of people and places are also capitalised.
⏱️
Punctuation End every sentence with a full stop (.), question mark (?), or exclamation mark (!). Don't forget commas (,).
🧩
Logical Order Write events in order: beginning – middle – end. Use First, Then, and Finally.
📖
Read It Again Always re-read your writing after you finish. Check spelling and punctuation!
🎨
Use Adjectives Add describing words to make your writing more vivid and interesting.
✏️
Write Neatly Neat writing is easier to read and shows your reader that you care about your work.
