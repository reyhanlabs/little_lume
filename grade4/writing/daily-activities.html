📌 Section 1

Building Sentences

A sentence is a group of words that gives a complete idea.

BASIC SENTENCE FORMULA

Subject+ Verb+ Object / Complement+ (Time / Place)

💡 A sentence always starts with a capital letter and ends with . ? or !

TypeExamplePattern
SimpleBudi plays football in the park.S + V + O + Place
SimpleShe eats rice every morning.S + V + O + Time
CompoundI like cats and she likes dogs.S+V+O + and/but + S+V+O
NegativeThey do not go to school today.S + do/does not + V
QuestionDo you like reading books?Do/Does + S + V + … ?

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌

"she play" → She plays (He/She/It + verb+s)

❌

"i am go" → I go to school (no double verb)

❌

"budi is my friend" → Budi (names = capital letter)

❌

Forgetting the full stop at the end of a sentence.

📌 Section 2

Writing a Paragraph

A paragraph is a group of sentences about one main idea.

🏷 Topic Sentence
States the main idea of the paragraph. It is the first sentence.
💬 Supporting 1
Gives the first detail or reason to support the main idea.
💬 Supporting 2
Gives more details, examples, or description.
💬 Supporting 3
Adds another fact, feeling, or example (optional for short paragraphs).
🔚 Closing
Wraps up the paragraph or restates the main idea in different words.
📄 Sample Paragraph ANNOTATED

My school is a wonderful place to learn. It has ten classrooms, a library, and a big sports field. The library has many colourful books in both English and Indonesian. Every morning, students sing the national anthem together in the yard. I am always happy to come to school every day.

🟡 Highlighted = Topic Sentence & Closing Sentence

📌 Section 3

Types of Writing Text

Grade 4 focuses on three text types. Each one has a different purpose.

🖼️ Descriptive Text

  • Purpose: Describe a person, animal, place, or object
  • Uses present tense
  • Uses adjectives (big, colourful, tall, sweet…)
  • Structure: Identification → Description

📖 Narrative Text

  • Purpose: Tell an imaginative story
  • Uses past tense
  • Has characters, setting, problem & solution
  • Structure: Orientation → Complication → Resolution

📅 Recount Text

  • Purpose: Retell a real past event
  • Uses past tense
  • Written in time order (first, next, after that…)
  • Structure: Orientation → Events → Re-orientation

💡 Quick Comparison

  • Descriptive → "My cat is fluffy."
  • Narrative → "The cat found a magic fish."
  • Recount → "Yesterday I played with my cat."
🖼️ Descriptive Text

Sample: My Pet Rabbit

Read and notice the structure and language features.

My Pet Rabbit DESCRIPTIVE

I have a pet rabbit named Bun.

Bun is a small and fluffy rabbit. He has white fur, two long ears, and a small pink nose. His eyes are bright red and very cute. Bun lives in a cosy cage in my bedroom.

Every morning, I give him fresh carrots and lettuce. He loves to jump around the room when I let him out. Bun is very gentle and never bites.

Bun is my favourite pet, and I take care of him every day.

Adjectives: small, fluffy, white, long, pink, bright, cosy, gentle, fresh Tense: Present Simple
📖 Narrative Text

Sample: The Brave Little Mouse

Read and notice Orientation → Complication → Resolution.

The Brave Little Mouse NARRATIVE

[Orientation] Once upon a time, there was a little mouse named Milo who lived in a cosy hole under a big oak tree in Sunny Forest.

[Complication] One day, Milo saw that a huge cat was chasing his best friend, Riko the rabbit. Riko was very scared and could not run fast enough. Milo did not know what to do.

Suddenly, Milo had a brilliant idea. He ran to the beehive nearby and shook it as hard as he could. The bees flew out and chased the cat away.

[Resolution] Riko hugged Milo tightly. "Thank you, brave Milo!" said Riko happily. From that day on, everyone in Sunny Forest knew that even the smallest friend can be the bravest hero.

Connectives: once upon a time, one day, suddenly, from that day on Tense: Past Simple
📅 Recount Text

Sample: My Trip to Borobudur

Read and notice Orientation → Events → Re-orientation.

My Trip to Borobudur RECOUNT

[Orientation] Last Sunday, my family and I visited Borobudur Temple in Magelang. The weather was sunny and beautiful.

[Events] First, we arrived at the entrance and bought tickets. Then, we climbed the stone stairs to the top of the temple. I saw many beautiful stone carvings on the walls. After that, we took lots of photos near the big stupas. My little sister was amazed by the tall Buddha statues. We had lunch at a small restaurant near the temple. The food was delicious.

[Re-orientation] Overall, the trip was wonderful and very educational. I hope we can visit Borobudur again next year.

Sequence words: first, then, after that, overall Action verbs: visited, climbed, saw, took, had Tense: Past Simple

Let's Write!

Complete the exercises below to practise your writing skills.

A Arrange the words into correct sentences.

  1. plays / football / every / Rina / afternoon
  2. books / we / in / read / the / library
  3. have / cat / a / I / orange / small
  4. they / do / not / like / vegetables
  5. your / what / is / favourite / subject

B Fill in the blanks with the correct word.

  1. My mother        breakfast every morning. (cook / cooks)
  2. Yesterday, I        to the zoo with my family. (go / went)
  3. The cat        big, brown eyes. (have / has)
  4.        you like ice cream? (Do / Does)
  5. I studied hard,        I got a good score. (and / but / so)

C Write a paragraph using these word prompts.

My best friend is tall kind always helps loves reading I am happy because

📝 Write at least 5 sentences. Include a topic sentence and a closing sentence.

D Choose ONE topic and write a short text (6–8 sentences).

  1. 🖼️ Descriptive: Describe your classroom or your school bag.
  2. 📖 Narrative: Write a short story about an animal that can talk.
  3. 📅 Recount: Write about a fun activity you did last weekend.

💡 Tip: Before writing, plan your text structure first!

💡 Writing Tips

Top 4 Writing Tips

Follow these steps every time you write!

1. PLAN ✏️Think about your main idea. Make a mind map or list of points before writing.
2. DRAFT 📝Write your ideas freely. Don't worry about mistakes yet — just write!
3. REVISE 🔍Re-read your writing. Add missing information or remove sentences that don't fit.
4. EDIT ✅Check spelling, grammar, punctuation, and capitalization carefully.

✅ Writing Checklist — Use before you submit!

☐

☐

☐

☐

☐

☐

☐
