🔊 Pronunciation Tips

Th

Put your tongue between your teeth.
👉 this, that, the, three

-ing

Let your nose hum at the end.
👉 playing, eating, going

Wh

Start with a soft "w" sound.
👉 what, where, when, who

-ed

Three ways: /t/, /d/, or /ɪd/.
👉 walked, played, wanted

R

Curl your tongue back slightly.
👉 read, run, really, right

V vs W

"v" bites the lip; "w" rounds lips.
👉 very / we, vine / wine

🎮 Speaking Activities

01

🌟 All About Me! (Self-Introduction)

Stand up and introduce yourself to the class for 30 seconds. Include your name, age, where you live, and your favorite hobby. Speak loudly and clearly — smile too! 😄

02

🤝 Find Your Match (Pair Interview)

Walk around the classroom and interview 3 classmates using these questions: "What's your favorite food?" "What do you do after school?" "Do you have a pet?" Then share your findings with the class.

03

🗺️ Help Me Get There! (Giving Directions)

Use the classroom map your teacher gives you. One student asks "How do I get to the library?" and the partner gives directions using: go straight, turn left / right, it's next to / between. Then swap!

04

🎭 Role Play – At the Market

One student is the seller, one is the buyer. Practice asking: "How much is this?" "I'd like two apples, please." "Here you are." "Thank you, have a nice day!" Use props or picture cards to make it fun!

📊 Speaking Assessment Rubric

Criteria Excellent (4) Good (3) Fair (2) Needs Work (1)
Fluency Speaks smoothly with no long pauses Mostly smooth, minor hesitations Several pauses, slow pace Very slow, frequent stops
Pronunciation Clear and easy to understand Mostly clear, minor errors Some unclear sounds Hard to understand
Vocabulary Uses varied and correct words Good word choice, few errors Limited vocabulary used Very basic words only
Confidence Eye contact, loud, confident Mostly confident, some shyness Quiet, looks at notes often Very shy, barely audible
Interaction Responds well, listens actively Usually responds correctly Needs prompting to respond Struggles to respond

Total: /20   18–20 = ⭐ Excellent  |  14–17 = 👍 Good  |  10–13 = 🙂 Fair  |  Below 10 = 📖 Keep Practicing!

📝 Teacher's Note

Encourage students to speak without fear of making mistakes — mistakes are part of learning! Use positive reinforcement (stickers, praise) after each activity. Pair stronger students with those who need more support. Allow students to use simple gestures and pictures if they get stuck. The goal at this stage is communication confidence, not perfection.
