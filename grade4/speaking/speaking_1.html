Useful Phrases

Important sentences you will use every lesson

🌅 Asking about daily routine:

What time do you wake up? What do you do after school? Do you always…?

✅ Answering about daily routine:

I wake up at six o'clock. I usually… after school. Yes, I always… / No, I never…

💬 Asking for help / clarification:

Can you repeat, please? What does … mean? I don't understand.

Model Dialogue

Read aloud first, then practice with a partner!

📖 Topic: My Daily Routine

🟦 Rafi (A)
Hi, Sari! What time do you usually wake up?
🟥 Sari (B)
I usually wake up at half past five.
🟦 Rafi (A)
Wow, that's early! What do you do in the morning?
🟥 Sari (B)
I pray, then I get dressed and have breakfast. After that, I go to school.
🟦 Rafi (A)
Do you always walk to school?
🟥 Sari (B)
No, I usually ride my bicycle. What about you?
🟦 Rafi (A)
I go by motorcycle with my dad. We leave at seven o'clock.

Classroom Activities

Three activities you will do in class today

🔄 Activity 1 – Interview a Friend (10 minutes · in pairs)

  • Interview your partner about their morning routine using the question phrases from Section 3.
  • Write down their answers, then share with the class: "My friend usually wakes up at…"
  • Use at least 5 different questions.

🎭 Activity 2 – Role Play (15 minutes · in pairs)

  • Act out the dialogue from Section 4, then change the details to match your own real routine.
  • Feel free to add your hobbies, favourite foods, or afternoon activities.
  • Perform in front of the class with a clear voice and great confidence!

🎙️ Activity 3 – One-Minute Talk (10 minutes · individual)

  • Describe your daily routine from morning to night for 1 full minute without stopping.
  • Start with: "Every day, I wake up at…"
  • Use these connecting words: first, then, after that, finally.

Grammar Spot

Simple Present Tense – for habits and daily routines

✅ Positive

I wake up at six.
She goes to school.
He plays football.

❌ Negative

I don't wake up late.
She doesn't walk.
He doesn't play games.

❓ Question

Do you wake up early? → Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
Does she go by bus? → Yes, she does. / No, she doesn't.
What time do you have breakfast?

⚠️

Remember! Add -s / -es for He, She, It:
go → goes | study → studies | play → plays
