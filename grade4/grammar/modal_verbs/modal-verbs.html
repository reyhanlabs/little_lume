What is a Modal Verb? 🤔

Your mission briefing starts here!

A modal verb is a special helper verb. It stands right next to a main verb and gives it a super power — like the power of ability, permission, possibility, or obligation.

Look at the pattern: Subject + Modal Verb + Main Verb (base form)
Example: I + can + swim. 🏊 No "s", no "-ing", no "to" — the main verb always stays in its simplest form!
🌟 The 7 Modal Verb Heroes you will meet today are: can, could, may, might, must, should, will (plus their partner shouldn't!). Scroll down to meet each one.
Meaning

Meet the Modal Verb Heroes 🦸

Each hero has one special job. Tap into their world!

💪

CAN

Power: Ability / Permission (informal)
"I can ride a bike." 🚲
"Can I open the window?"
🕰️

COULD

Power: Past ability / Polite request
"When I was 5, I could not read." 📖
"Could you help me, please?"
🙋

MAY

Power: Polite permission / Possibility
"May I go to the toilet?" 🚪
"It may rain today." 🌧️
🌫️

MIGHT

Power: Small possibility (not sure!)
"I might visit Grandma this weekend." 👵
🚨

MUST

Power: Strong rule / Necessity
"You must wear a helmet." 🪖
"We must be quiet in the library." 🤫
💡

SHOULD

Power: Good advice / Suggestion
"You should drink more water." 💧
🚫

SHOULDN'T

Power: Advice to NOT do something
"You shouldn't eat too much candy." 🍬
🔮

WILL

Power: Future / Promise
"I will call you tonight." 📞
"It will be sunny tomorrow." ☀️
Remember

5 Super Important Rules 📝

Every hero follows the same rulebook!

  • 1 Modal verbs NEVER change for he/she/it. ❌ "She cans swim." ✅ "She can swim."
  • 2 The verb after a modal is always in its BASE form (no "to", no "-ing", no "-s"). ✅ "He must go." not "must to go" or "must going".
  • 3 To make a question, just swap the order: Modal + Subject + Verb? ✅ "Can you dance?"
  • 4 To make a negative, add "not" right after the modal: cannot (can't), shouldn't, mustn't.
  • 5 Choose the right hero for the right job: ability = can/could, permission = can/may, advice = should, rule = must, possibility = might/may, future = will.
