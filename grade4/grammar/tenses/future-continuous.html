🚀 What is Future Continuous Tense? Core Concept

Definition

The Future Continuous Tense is used to talk about actions that will be happening in the future — during a specific moment or period in the future. It shows what will be going on!

Think of it as looking ahead with a crystal ball — it shows you actions that will be actively happening at a moment in the future!

Compare with Other Tenses

🔀 Present Continuous vs. Simple Present

🔹 Simple Future

  • I will play football.
  • She will read a book.
  • We will eat lunch.

One action in the future — but we don't know how long it will happen.

🔸 Future Continuous

  • I will be playing football.
  • She will be reading a book.
  • We will be eating lunch.

Action in progress in the future — it will be happening then!

🦊
BunRey Fox says: Future Continuous uses will be + -ing. It's the same for all subjects! Use it when you want to describe what will be happening at a specific time in the future!
🔨 Form & Formula How to Build It

The Magic Formula

Future Continuous Tense uses will be + the -ing form of the verb. The formula is the same for all subjects!

Formula

📐 Future Continuous Formula

Subject + will be + Verb + -ing
Example: She + will be + reading = "She will be reading."
For All Subjects

Important Rule

✏️ Adding "-ing" to Verbs

Most verbs just add -ing, but some have special rules:

  • play → playing ✓
  • read → reading ✓
  • stop → stopping (double the last letter!) ✓
  • make → making (drop the 'e' first!) ✓
  • hop → hopping (double the 'p'!) ✓
⏰ When to Use Present Continuous Usage Guide

Perfect Moments to Use It

Use Present Continuous when describing actions happening RIGHT NOW at this very moment, or actions in progress during a specific time period.

Situation 1

📹 Actions Happening Now

Right now, at this moment:

  • I am writing an essay.
  • She is studying for the test.
  • They are playing in the park.
Situation 2

📍 Temporary Actions

Actions that are temporary or in progress:

  • I am learning English this year.
  • He is building a house at the moment.
  • We are preparing for the concert.
Signal Words

🎯 Words That Go With Present Continuous

These words often appear with Present Continuous:

  • now — I am eating now.
  • right now — She is running right now.
  • at the moment — They are playing at the moment.
  • this week / month / year — We are studying this week.
  • Look! — Look! He is jumping!
❌ Negative & ❓ Question Forms Structure Guide
Negative Form

❌ How to Say "NOT Doing"

Subject + am/is/are + NOT + Verb + -ing
Example: I + am not + playing = "I am not playing."
PositiveNegative
I am reading.I am not reading.
She is playing.She is not playing.
They are running.They are not running.
Question Form

❓ How to Ask Questions

Am/Is/Are + Subject + Verb + -ing ?
Example: Is + she + reading? = "Is she reading?"
QuestionShort Answer (Yes)Short Answer (No)
Am I playing?Yes, you are.No, you aren't.
Is she reading?Yes, she is.No, she isn't.
Are they running?Yes, they are.No, they aren't.
💬 Real Dialogue Examples Practice Listening
👦 Boy (Rini):
"Hi, Sinta! What are you doing right now?"
👧 Girl (Sinta):
"Hi, Rini! I am playing with my cat. It is running around the room!"
👦 Boy (Rini):
"That sounds fun! Are you coming to the park tomorrow?"
👧 Girl (Sinta):
"Yes! But right now, we are eating lunch. Are you eating too?"
👦 Boy (Rini):
"No, I'm not. I am watching my favorite show. But I am getting hungry!"
💡
Notice: In this conversation, every sentence talks about actions that are happening RIGHT NOW — playing, running, coming, eating, watching, getting!
