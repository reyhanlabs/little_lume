Useful Phrases

Sentences you will use to talk about location

❓ Asking about location:

Where is the...? Where are the...? Is the... on/in/under...?

✅ Answering about location:

It's on the... It's in the... They are next to the... Yes, it is. / No, it isn't.

💬 Giving directions:

Go straight, then turn left. It's between the... and the...

Model Dialogue

Read aloud first, then practice with a partner!

📖 Topic: Where Is My Bag?

🟦 Dani (A)
Mom, where is my bag? I can't find it!
🟥 Mom (B)
Is it under your bed?
🟦 Dani (A)
No, it isn't. I already checked.
🟥 Mom (B)
Look! It's behind the chair, next to your shoes.
🟦 Dani (A)
Oh, I see it now! Thank you, Mom!
🟥 Mom (B)
You're welcome. Now put your books in your bag, okay?
🟦 Dani (A)
Okay! My pencil case is on the table. I'll put it in my bag too.

6

Classroom Activities

Three activities you will do in class today

📦 Activity 1 – Hide and Describe (10 minutes · in pairs)

  • One student hides a small object (eraser, pencil) somewhere in the classroom.
  • Their partner asks: "Where is the eraser?"
  • The first student answers using a preposition: "It's under the desk."
  • Take turns and try to use a different preposition each time.

🎨 Activity 2 – Draw and Label (15 minutes · individual)

  • Draw a simple picture of your bedroom or classroom.
  • Write 5 sentences describing where things are, using different prepositions.
  • Example: "My book is on the shelf. My shoes are under the chair."
  • Share your drawing and sentences with a partner.

🗣️ Activity 3 – Classroom Treasure Hunt (15 minutes · whole class)

  • The teacher gives clues using prepositions: "I am thinking of something that is on the wall, near the door."
  • Students guess the object (e.g. "Is it the clock?").
  • Then, students take turns giving their own clues for classmates to guess.
7

Grammar Spot

How to use prepositions of place in a sentence

📐 Sentence Pattern

Subject + is / are + Preposition + the + Noun

The ball is on the floor.
The books are in the bag.
The dog is under the table.

❓ Question Pattern

Where is the cat? → It's on the sofa.
Where are my shoes? → They're under the bed.
Is the cup on the table? → Yes, it is. / No, it isn't.

⚠️

Remember! Use is for singular (one thing) and are for plural (more than one):
The pen is on the desk. → The pens are on the desk.
