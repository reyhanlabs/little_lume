✨ Articles: A, An & The ✨

1🤔 What Are Articles?

Articles are small but super important words that go before nouns.
They help us know which thing we're talking about — or how many!

An
The

2🌀 Indefinite Articles: A & An
🔴 Use A before words that start with a consonant sound.
(b, c, d, f, g, h, j, k, l, m, n, p, q, r, s, t, v, w, x, y, z)
a cat 🐱 a ball ⚽ a dog 🐶 a house 🏠 a tree 🌳 a uniform 👕
🔵 Use An before words that start with a vowel sound.
(a, e, i, o, u)
an apple 🍎 an egg 🥚 an ice cream 🍦 an orange 🍊 an umbrella ☂️ an hour ⏰
💡 Fun Tip! "A uniform" uses A because "uniform" sounds like YOO-niform (starts with a Y sound, not a vowel sound!). And "An hour" uses AN because the H is silent — it sounds like OW-er!
3🎯 Definite Article: The
🟢 Use The when you're talking about something specific that both the speaker and listener know about.

📖 Look at these examples:

🐱 I saw a cat in the garden. The cat was black.
🏫 I go to a school near my house. The school has a big playground.
🌞 The sun is bright today. (There is only one sun!)
🌍 The Earth goes around the sun.
the moon 🌙 the president 🎖️ the book I like 📖 the tallest mountain 🏔️
