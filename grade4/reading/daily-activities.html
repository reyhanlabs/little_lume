Reading Strategy

🧠

Before, During & After Reading

Good readers use strategies every step of the way. Try these tricks to understand any text:

  • Before: Look at the title and pictures. Ask yourself — "What is this about?"
  • During: Read slowly. Stop and re-read if something is confusing.
  • During: Circle words you don't know. Use context clues to guess the meaning.
  • After: Ask yourself — "What was the most important idea?"
  • After: Retell the text in your own words to a friend or teacher.

Reading Passage

🐘

The Amazing Elephants of the Forest

Deep in the ancient rainforest of Borneo, there lives a very special animal — the Borneo pygmy elephant. These gentle giants are the enormous wonders of the jungle, yet they are the smallest elephants in Asia. A fully grown pygmy elephant is about 2.5 metres tall, which is much shorter than African elephants.

Scientists became curious about how these elephants arrived in Borneo thousands of years ago. After many years of research, they discovered that the elephants were brought to the island as gifts for a sultan long ago. When the sultan no longer wanted them, the elephants were set free into the forest. Over many generations, they changed and adapted to their new habitat.

Today, pygmy elephants face many dangers. Logging companies cut down the forest where the elephants live. Without trees, the elephants lose their food and their home. There are fewer than 1,500 of these elephants left in the wild. Many organisations work hard to protect these animals so that they can survive for many years to come.

If you ever visit Borneo, you might be lucky enough to see a herd of pygmy elephants on their daily journey through the forest in search of food and water. They move quietly and peacefully, reminding us how important it is to care for the amazing animals that share our world.

Classroom Activities

🗺️ Story Map

Draw a story map with four boxes:

  • Who is in the text?
  • Where does it take place?
  • What is the problem?
  • What is the solution?

🔤 Vocabulary Match

Cut out the 8 vocabulary words. Match each word to its definition on a separate card. Time yourself — can you beat your classmates?

🎨 Draw & Label

Draw a pygmy elephant in its habitat. Label at least 5 things you can see. Use vocabulary words from the lesson in your labels.

📢 Read Aloud Partner

Take turns reading one paragraph each with a partner. After each paragraph, tell your partner one thing you learned.

📰 Write a Headline

Imagine you are a journalist. Write a newspaper headline and two sentences about pygmy elephants. Make it exciting!

🔎 Find the Clues

Go back and underline:

  • Numbers (in blue)
  • Adjectives (in green)
  • Action verbs (in red)
