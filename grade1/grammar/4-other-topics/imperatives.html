📖 Introduction to Imperatives Starter

What Are Imperatives?

Imperatives are sentences that tell someone to do something. They give commands, instructions, or requests. We use imperatives every day at home, at school, and with our friends!

When Do We Use Imperatives?

Imperatives are useful for many things:

Give commands
Sit down! Stand up! Listen!
Give instructions
Raise your hand! Open your books!
Make requests
Please help me! Can you pass the salt?
Make invitations
Come play with us! Join our game!
Give advice
Eat your vegetables! Study hard!

Fun Fact! 🌟

Imperatives start with a verb and do not use pronouns like "you." They are short, direct, and show strong feeling or urgency!

🎯 Imperatives Pattern Structure

The Basic Structure

Imperatives are special sentences. They start directly with the verb, without using "you." This makes them quick and powerful!

Verb + Object/Details + !
Examples: Listen! | Close the door! | Sit down!

Two Types of Imperatives

Positive: Tell someone TO do something (Open the door!)
Negative: Tell someone NOT to do something (Don't run!)

✏️ Using Imperatives Practice

Positive Imperatives - At Home

Use imperatives to give commands or instructions at home:

Example 1:
Go to bed!
Example 2:
Wash your hands!
Example 3:
Eat your dinner!
Example 4:
Clean your room!

Positive Imperatives - At School

Teachers and students use imperatives in the classroom:

Example 1:
Open your books!
Example 2:
Raise your hand!
Example 3:
Sit down!
Example 4:
Listen carefully!

Negative Imperatives - Using "Don't"

Use "Don't" to tell people NOT to do something:

Example 1:
Don't run in the classroom!
Example 2:
Don't touch that!
Example 3:
Don't eat candy before dinner!
Example 4:
Don't be late!

Polite Imperatives - With "Please"

We can make imperatives more polite by adding "please":

Example 1:
Please sit down!
Example 2:
Please help me!
Example 3:
Please be quiet!
Example 4:
Please close the window!

💡 Real Examples Stories

Example 1: Classroom Command

Sentence:
Stand up, everyone!
What it shows:
The teacher is telling students to stand.

Example 2: Reminder

Sentence:
Don't forget your homework!
What it shows:
A reminder not to forget something important.

Example 3: Polite Request

Sentence:
Please turn off the light!
What it shows:
A polite request to turn off the light.

Example 4: Instructions

Sentence:
Mix the flour and eggs together!
What it shows:
Instructions for making something.

Example 5: Safety Command

Sentence:
Stop! Look both ways!
What it shows:
Important safety instructions.

Example 6: Friendly Invitation

Sentence:
Come play with us!
What it shows:
A friendly invitation to join an activity.

💬 Practice Dialogue Speaking

Teacher:
Good morning, class! Sit down, please!
Teacher:
Everyone, open your books to page 10!
Student 1:
Teacher, I can't find my book!
Teacher:
Don't worry! Look in your desk!
Student 2:
Teacher, what do we do next?
Teacher:
Read the story carefully! Then answer the questions on the paper!
Teacher:
And remember - don't make noise!
All Students:
Okay, Teacher!
Student 3 (After 10 minutes):
Teacher, we finished!
Teacher:
Great! Bring me your papers! Everyone, please clean up now!
