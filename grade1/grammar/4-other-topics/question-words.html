📖 Introduction to Question Words Starter

What Are Question Words?

Question words are special words that help us ask questions. They ask about who, what, where, when, why, and how. These words are very important for asking and answering questions in English!

The Six Main Question Words

We use these question words every day to get information:

Who?
Asks about people - Who is your friend?
What?
Asks about things - What do you like?
Where?
Asks about places - Where is your school?
When?
Asks about time - When is your birthday?
Why?
Asks about reasons - Why do you like pizza?
How?
Asks about manner - How are you today?

Fun Fact! 🌟

Question words always come at the beginning of the question! This helps people understand what information you're looking for!

🎯 Question Words Pattern Structure

The Basic Pattern

When we ask questions with question words, we follow a simple pattern. The question word comes first, then the verb and subject!

Question Word + Verb + Subject + ?
Example: Where do you live?

Each Question Word Has Its Purpose

Different question words help us get different information. When you want to know about people, use "Who?". When you want to know about objects, use "What?". This makes communication easy and clear!

✏️ Using Question Words Practice

WHO? - Asking About People

Use "Who" when you want to know about a person:

Example 1:
Who is your best friend?
Example 2:
Who plays soccer with you?
Example 3:
Who can help me?

WHAT? - Asking About Things

Use "What" when you want to know about objects or things:

Example 1:
What is your name?
Example 2:
What do you like to eat?
Example 3:
What is your favorite color?

WHERE? - Asking About Places

Use "Where" when you want to know about location:

Example 1:
Where do you live?
Example 2:
Where is the bathroom?
Example 3:
Where are your shoes?

WHEN? - Asking About Time

Use "When" when you want to know about time:

Example 1:
When is your birthday?
Example 2:
When do we have lunch?
Example 3:
When is the party?

WHY? - Asking About Reasons

Use "Why" when you want to know the reason:

Example 1:
Why do you like ice cream?
Example 2:
Why are you happy?
Example 3:
Why is the sky blue?

HOW? - Asking About Manner

Use "How" when you want to know how something is done:

Example 1:
How are you today?
Example 2:
How do you spell your name?
Example 3:
How do you make a sandwich?

💬 Practice Dialogue Speaking

Teacher:
Good morning, class! Today we have a new student. Her name is Sofia.
Student 1:
Who is Sofia? Where is she from?
Teacher:
Sofia is from Canada! She just moved here.
Student 2:
What do you like to do, Sofia?
Sofia:
I like to play soccer and read books!
Student 3:
When do you play soccer?
Sofia:
I play soccer every Saturday afternoon!
Student 1:
How do you like our school?
Sofia:
I like it very much! Everyone is so friendly. Why are you all so nice?
All Students:
Because we like making new friends! Welcome to our class, Sofia!
