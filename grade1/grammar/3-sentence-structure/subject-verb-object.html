📖 Introduction to SVO Starter

What is a Sentence?

A sentence is a group of words that makes a complete thought. Most English sentences follow a simple pattern: Subject - Verb - Object. This is called SVO word order.

The Three Parts

Every simple sentence has three main parts:

Subject
Who or what is doing the action?
Verb
What action is happening?
Object
Who or what receives the action?

Fun Fact! 🌟

In English, the subject always comes first! This is different from some other languages. The order Subject-Verb-Object is very important in English!

🎯 SVO Pattern Structure

The Subject - Verb - Object Pattern

Most English sentences follow this pattern. Understanding this helps us write and speak better English!

SUBJECT
+
VERB
+
OBJECT
= Complete Sentence

1. SUBJECT - Who or What? 🧑

The subject is the person or thing doing the action. It comes first in the sentence.

Examples: I, you, he, she, it, they, the cat, my friend, the ball

2. VERB - The Action 🏃

The verb is the action word. It tells us what the subject is doing.

Examples: eat, play, run, jump, read, like, love, have

3. OBJECT - Receives the Action 🎁

The object receives the action. It comes after the verb. Not all sentences have objects!

Examples: ball, book, apple, ice cream, homework, friend

📝 Complete Sentences (SVO)

  • I eat apples
  • She reads books
  • They play soccer

❓ Parts Labeled

  • Subject: I, She, They
  • Verb: eat, reads, play
  • Object: apples, books, soccer

✏️ How to Build Sentences Practice

Building Sentences Step by Step

To make a sentence, we put Subject + Verb + Object together in the right order.

1. Find the Subject 🧑

Ask: Who or what is doing something?

Example: The girl... (subject)

2. Find the Verb 🏃

Ask: What is the subject doing?

Example: The girl plays... (verb = plays)

3. Find the Object 🎁

Ask: What or who receives the action?

Example: The girl plays soccer. (object = soccer)

4. Check the Order 📍

Make sure it's Subject - Verb - Object!

✓ Correct: The girl plays soccer.
✗ Wrong: Plays the girl soccer.

Sentences with People as Objects 👥

Subject: I | Verb: love | Object: my mother

They like me

We see them

Sentences with Animals as Objects 🐾

Sentences without Objects 🚀

Subject: I | Verb: run | (No object)

Subject: They | Verb: jump | (No object)
