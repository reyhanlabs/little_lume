📖 Introduction to Statements & Questions Starter

Two Ways to Communicate

In English, we have two main types of sentences: statements (telling information) and questions (asking for information). They have different word orders and punctuation!

The Two Types

Statements (.)
Tell us information - I like pizza.
Questions (?)
Ask for information - Do you like pizza?

Fun Fact! 🌟

The punctuation is different! Statements end with a period (.). Questions end with a question mark (?). This helps us understand if someone is telling us or asking us something!

🎯 Statements vs Questions Structure

Different Word Order for Questions

Statements and questions use DIFFERENT word orders. This is important!

STATEMENT
:
Subject + Verb
I like pizza.
QUESTION
:
Verb + Subject
Do you like pizza?

1. Statements (.) 📝

Statements tell us something. They follow normal Subject-Verb-Object word order and end with a period.

Examples: I like pizza. She plays soccer. They have dogs.

2. Questions (?) ❓

Questions ask for information. They often start with a helper verb and end with a question mark.

Examples: Do you like pizza? Does she play soccer? Do they have dogs?

📝 STATEMENTS

  • I am happy
  • She plays soccer
  • They like apples
  • It is big

❓ QUESTIONS

  • Are you happy?
  • Does she play soccer?
  • Do they like apples?
  • Is it big?

✏️ How to Form Questions Practice

Making Questions from Statements

To make a question from a statement, we often move the helper verb to the beginning!

1. With "Be" Verbs (am, is, are) 🌟

Move "am/is/are" to the beginning!

Statement: You are happy.
Question: Are you happy?

2. With "Do/Does" ✅

Add "Do/Does" at the beginning!

Statement: You like pizza.
Question: Do you like pizza?

3. With Question Words ❓

Use What, Where, Who, When, Why, How at the beginning!

Examples: What do you like? Where do you live? Who are you?

4. Punctuation Matters! 📍

Always end questions with a question mark (?) and statements with a period (.)

Statement: You like pizza. (period)
Question: Do you like pizza? (question mark)
