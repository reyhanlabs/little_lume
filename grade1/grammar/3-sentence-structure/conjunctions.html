📖 Introduction to Conjunctions Starter

What are Conjunctions?

Conjunctions are words that connect other words or sentences together. They help us join ideas! The most common conjunctions are: AND, OR, BUT.

The Main Conjunctions

AND
Adds similar ideas - I like apples AND oranges
OR
Gives choices - Do you like tea OR coffee?
BUT
Shows contrast - I like soccer BUT I don't like running

Fun Fact! 🌟

Conjunctions are like bridges between words and ideas. They help us build longer, more interesting sentences!

🎯 The Three Main Conjunctions Structure

Three Important Joining Words

These three conjunctions are the most important to learn at Grade 1!

1. AND (Addition) 🤝

Use AND to add similar ideas or list things together.

Examples: I like apples AND oranges. She reads AND writes. They jump AND run.

2. OR (Choice) ❓

Use OR to give two choices or alternatives.

Examples: Do you like tea OR coffee? You can read OR play. It's hot OR cold.

3. BUT (Contrast) 🙅

Use BUT to show opposite or contrasting ideas.

Examples: I like pizza BUT not fish. She is smart BUT lazy. They like sports BUT not music.

AND Connection

  • apple AND orange
  • swim AND play
  • smart AND kind

BUT Contrast

  • big BUT slow
  • happy BUT scared
  • like BUT can't

✏️ Using Conjunctions Practice

How to Use Conjunctions

Conjunctions connect two ideas or words of the same type (noun + noun, adjective + adjective, sentence + sentence).

Connecting Two Nouns 📝

Noun AND Noun: apples AND oranges | cats AND dogs | reading AND writing

Connecting Two Adjectives 🎨

Adjective AND Adjective: big AND small | happy AND sad | fast BUT slow

Connecting Two Sentences 📚

Sentence + Conjunction + Sentence: I like soccer AND I play every day. She likes reading BUT she doesn't like writing.

Pattern: Word + Conjunction + Word 🔗

The pattern is always the same: Word + AND/OR/BUT + Word

💡 Real Examples Learn

AND - Addition 🤝

Two nouns
I like apples AND oranges
Two nouns
She has cats AND dogs
Two verbs
I read AND write every day
Two sentences
I like soccer AND I play it well

OR - Choice ❓

Two nouns
Do you like tea OR coffee?
Two nouns
You can choose a book OR a movie
Two verbs
Will you read OR play?
Two sentences
We can go today OR tomorrow

BUT - Contrast 🙅

Two adjectives
The box is big BUT light
Two ideas
I like swimming BUT not diving
Contrast
She is smart BUT she doesn't like math
Contrast
They are fast runners BUT slow swimmers

💬 Dialogue Practice Speaking

I like apples AND oranges! Both are yummy!

Do you like sports? Soccer OR basketball?

I like soccer BUT basketball is too hard for me.
👧 Sophie:

👦 Max:
Great! I love playing with you!
