📖 Introduction to Personal Pronouns Starter

What is a Personal Pronoun?

A personal pronoun is a word that REPLACES a noun. Instead of saying "John" every time, we say "he". Pronouns help us avoid repeating the same noun over and over!

Why Do We Need Personal Pronouns?

Pronouns make our sentences shorter and less repetitive. Without pronouns, we would have to repeat names and nouns constantly!

❌ Without Pronouns:
"John is happy. John plays games. John likes ice cream."
✅ With Pronouns:
"John is happy. He plays games. He likes ice cream."

Five Main Personal Pronouns 🎯

In Grade 1, we learn five important pronouns that replace different people and things:

I (me) | You (you) | He (him) | She (her) | It (it)

🎯 Pronoun Concepts Structure

The Five Personal Pronouns Explained

Each pronoun replaces a specific type of person or thing. Learning which pronoun to use is important!

Pronoun Meaning Example
I Talking about yourself I am happy. I play games.
You Talking to someone You are smart. You play well.
He A boy or man He is John. He runs fast.
She A girl or woman She is Sarah. She sings songs.
It An animal or thing It is a dog. It runs away.

1. I - Talking About Yourself

Use "I" when you are talking about yourself. You are the one doing something.

Examples: I am happy. I like apples. I read books. I play with my friends.

2. You - Talking to Someone

Use "you" when you talk directly to another person. You are speaking TO them.

Examples: You are smart. You like ice cream. You play well. You are my friend.

3. He - A Boy or Man

Use "he" when talking about a boy or man. He replaces a boy's or man's name.

Examples: He is my brother. He plays football. He likes chocolate. He runs fast.

4. She - A Girl or Woman

Use "she" when talking about a girl or woman. She replaces a girl's or woman's name.

Examples: She is my sister. She plays piano. She reads books. She sings songs.

5. It - An Animal or Thing

Use "it" when talking about an animal or object. It replaces the name of an animal or thing.

Examples: It is a dog. It is red. It runs fast. It is beautiful.

✏️ How to Use Personal Pronouns Practice

Position of Pronouns in Sentences

Personal pronouns usually come at the BEGINNING of a sentence or after a verb, just like nouns.

Where Pronouns Go

At the Start:
I play games. | You are smart. | He runs fast.
After Verbs:
This is him. | I see you. | She likes it.
Replacing Names:
John plays → He plays | Sarah reads → She reads

Pronoun Changes in Sentences

Some pronouns change form depending on how they're used in a sentence:

I (subject) → me (object) | He (subject) → him (object) | She (subject) → her (object)

Subject vs Object Pronouns

Subject pronouns do the action: I run, You play, He eats, She sings, It barks.

Object pronouns receive the action: Give it to me. I see you. She likes him.

Pronouns in Everyday Sentences

About Yourself:

Talking to Someone:

About Boys/Men:

🎯 Pronoun Replacement Examples

Sarah reads a book. → She reads a book.

John likes apples. → He likes apples.

The cat plays. → It plays.

You are happy. → You are happy. (already a pronoun!)

I eat lunch. → I eat lunch. (already a pronoun!)

🔍 Find the Pronouns!

Look at this sentence: "He is my friend. She likes him. They like it."

Pronouns: He, She, him, it

Not pronouns: is (verb), my (possessive), friend (noun), likes (verb)

💬 Practice Dialogue Interactive

Using Pronouns in Conversations

Here's how we use pronouns naturally when we talk. Notice how pronouns replace names!

👧 Maya:
Hi Tom! Where are you going?
👦 Tom:
I am going to the park. Do you want to come?
👧 Maya:
Sure! Is your sister coming too?
👦 Tom:
Yes, she is coming. She loves the park! It is very fun there.
👧 Maya:
Great! I love the park too. It is my favorite place!

Answer: 6 pronouns! Can you identify what each one replaces?
