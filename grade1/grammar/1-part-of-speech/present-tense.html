📖 Introduction to Simple Present Tense Starter

What is Simple Present Tense?

Simple Present Tense is used to talk about actions that happen NOW or actions that we do REGULARLY. It's the most common tense for talking about everyday activities!

Why Do We Need Simple Present Tense?

Simple Present helps us describe what we do every day, what happens regularly, and what is true right now. It's essential for talking about our daily lives!

Regular Actions:
I eat breakfast every morning. She plays games after school.
Things That Are True:
The sun rises in the east. I like ice cream.
Current Habitual Actions:
He studies English every day. They play football on weekends.

Simple Present = Base Verb Form 🎯

Simple Present uses the base form of verbs (without adding -ed). Sometimes we add -s or -es for he, she, and it.

Example: I play | You play | He plays (with -s) | She plays | It runs

🎯 Simple Present Concept Structure

The Pattern of Simple Present

Simple Present has a basic pattern. Understanding this pattern helps us use verbs correctly!

SUBJECT
+
BASE VERB
or +s
= Simple Present Tense
The verb stays the same for most subjects, except he/she/it adds -s
Subject Verb Form Example
I Base verb (no -s) I play football
You Base verb (no -s) You play football
He Base verb + s He plays football
She Base verb + s She plays football
It Base verb + s It runs fast
We Base verb (no -s) We play football
They Base verb (no -s) They play football

Pattern 1: Subject + Verb

The simplest pattern is: Subject + Verb

Examples: I run. You eat. He plays. She sings. It barks.

Pattern 2: Subject + Verb + Object

Many sentences include what we are doing: Subject + Verb + Object

Examples: I eat an apple. You read books. He plays games. She sings songs.

Adding -S for He/She/It

Remember: Only he, she, and it need the -s at the end of the verb!

I play but He plays | You eat but She eats | They run but It runs

✏️ How to Use Simple Present Practice

When to Use Simple Present

Use Simple Present for actions that happen regularly, habits, or things that are always true.

Uses of Simple Present

Daily Habits:
I brush my teeth every morning. She goes to school at 7 AM.
Regular Actions:
He plays football on Saturdays. They eat lunch at noon.
General Truths:
The sun rises in the east. Water is cold. I like apples.
Frequency Words:
I always eat breakfast. She never drinks coffee. We usually play games.

Frequency Words with Simple Present

These words help us talk about how often we do things:

Always (100%) | Usually (often) | Sometimes (not always) | Never (0%)

Basic Word Order

Subject + Frequency Word + Verb + Object

Example: I always eat breakfast. She usually plays games. We never go to bed late.

💡 Real Examples Learning

Simple Present in Daily Life

Morning:
I wake up at 7 AM. I brush my teeth. I eat breakfast. I go to school.
At School:
I study English. We play games. The teacher teaches us. I learn new things.
After School:
I play with friends. I do homework. I eat snacks. I watch TV.
Evening:
I eat dinner. I play with my family. I read books. I go to bed.

🎯 Sample Sentences

1. I eat breakfast every morning.

2. She plays football on weekends.

3. He goes to school every day.

4. They like ice cream.

5. It rains sometimes.

🔍 Positive and Negative

Positive: I like apples. He plays games. She eats ice cream.

Negative: I don't like apples. He doesn't play games. She doesn't eat ice cream.

💬 Practice Dialogue Interactive

Using Simple Present in Conversations

Here's how we use Simple Present when we talk about our daily lives and habits!

👧 Lisa:
What do you do every day?
👦 David:
I wake up at 7 AM. I eat breakfast, then I go to school.
👧 Lisa:
What do you do at school?
👦 David:
I study English and math. My friends and I play games at lunch. After school, I play football.
👧 Lisa:
That sounds fun! What do you do in the evening?
👦 David:
I do homework, eat dinner, and play with my family. Then I go to bed at 9 PM.
