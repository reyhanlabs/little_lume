📖 Introduction to Plurals Starter

What is a Plural?

A plural is when we have MORE THAN ONE of something. We make words plural by adding -S to the end. For example: one cat becomes two cats!

Singular vs Plural

Singular means ONE thing. Plural means MORE THAN ONE (2, 3, 4, 5, etc.). In English, we usually add -S to make plurals!

Singular → Plural:
one cat → two cats | one book → three books | one apple → many apples

How Do We Make Plurals? 🎯

For most nouns in Grade 1, we simply add -S to the end of the word!

Rule: Singular Noun + S = Plural Noun

✏️ How to Use Plurals Practice

When to Use Plurals

Use the plural form when you are talking about TWO or MORE of something. Use singular when talking about ONE.

Singular vs Plural in Sentences

With Numbers:
I have 1 apple (singular). I have 5 apples (plural).
With "a" or "the":
I see a cat (singular). I see cats (plural, no "a").
With Verbs:
The cat is playing. The cats are playing.

Rule: When to Add -S

Add -S to MOST nouns to make them plural:

cat → cats | dog → dogs | book → books | toy → toys | apple → apples | flower → flowers

Important: Verbs Change Too!

When a noun is plural, sometimes the verb changes:

One cat IS happy. | Two cats ARE happy. | One book IS big. | Three books ARE big.

🔍 Find the Plurals!

Look at this sentence: "I have two cats. They sleep on three beds. I give them five toys."

Plurals: cats, beds, toys

Not plurals: I (pronoun), have (verb), two (number), sleep (verb)

💬 Practice Dialogue Interactive

Talking About Multiple Things

Here's how we use plurals when we talk about having many things. Notice how the plural forms are used!

👧 Maya:
How many books do you have?
👦 Alex:
I have ten books! They are on my shelves.
👧 Maya:
Wow! Do you have any toys?
👦 Alex:
Yes! I have five toys. There are cars, balls, and dolls.
👧 Maya:
That's great! How many friends do you have?
👦 Alex:
I have many friends! We play games together!

📢 Plural Counting

How many plurals are in the dialogue?

books, shelves, toys, cars, balls, dolls, friends

Answer: 7 plural nouns!
