📖 Introduction to Articles Starter

What is an Article?

An article is a small word that goes BEFORE a noun to show if it is specific or general. The three articles are: a, an, and the. Articles are very common in English!

Why Do We Need Articles?

Articles help us know if we are talking about something specific or something in general. They also help us speak correctly and clearly!

❌ Without Article:
"I eat apple." (sounds strange)
✅ With Article:
"I eat an apple." (sounds correct)

The Three Articles 📚

In Grade 1, we learn three main articles that we use before nouns:

A (before consonant sounds) | An (before vowel sounds) | The (specific things)

🎯 Article Concepts Structure

The Three Articles Explained

Each article has a special use. Understanding when to use each one is important!

Article When to Use Example
A Before consonant sounds (b, c, d, f, g, etc.) a book, a dog, a cat, a pen
An Before vowel sounds (a, e, i, o, u) an apple, an egg, an orange, an umbrella
The For things we already know about the book, the dog, the school

1. "A" for Consonant Sounds

Use "a" before words that start with consonant sounds. Consonants are: b, c, d, f, g, h, j, k, l, m, n, p, r, s, t, v, w, x, y, z.

Examples: a ball, a cat, a dog, a friend, a house, a pen, a toy, a zebra

2. "An" for Vowel Sounds

Use "an" before words that start with vowel sounds. Vowels are: a, e, i, o, u.

Examples: an apple, an egg, an ice cream, an orange, an umbrella, an ant, an elephant, an igloo

3. "The" for Specific Things

Use "the" when talking about a specific noun that we know about. Use "the" with both consonants and vowels!

Examples: the book, the apple, the dog, the school, the teacher, the cat, the playground

🟢 Correct (Articles)

  • A book
  • An apple
  • The cat
  • A dog

🔴 Without Articles

  • Book (sounds strange)
  • Apple (sounds strange)
  • Cat (sounds strange)
  • Dog (sounds strange)

✏️ How to Use Articles Practice

Position of Articles in Sentences

Articles always come BEFORE the noun. Usually after the subject but before adjectives and nouns.

Where Articles Go

Article + Noun:
a book | an apple | the dog
Article + Adjective + Noun:
a big house | an orange ball | the happy cat
In Complete Sentences:
I see a bird. She has an umbrella. He likes the game.

A vs An - The Sound Rule

Remember: It's about the SOUND, not the spelling!

a hat (starts with 'h' sound) | an hour (starts with vowel sound) | a university (starts with 'y' sound)

When to Use Each Article

First time mentioning something: Use "a" or "an"

Second time or something specific: Use "the"

Example: I see a dog. The dog is brown.

💡 Real Examples Learning

Articles in Everyday Life

At Home:
I have a bed. The bed is soft. I see an apple. The apple is red.
At School:
I have a pencil. The pencil is blue. I see a teacher. The teacher is kind.
At Play:
I play with a ball. The ball is big. I see a butterfly. The butterfly is pretty.

🎯 Sentences with Articles

1. I eat an apple for breakfast.

2. The apple is sweet and juicy.

3. I have a cat at home.

4. The cat is orange and fluffy.

5. She is a good friend.

🔍 Find the Articles!

Look at this sentence: "I see a dog. The dog is playing with a ball."

Articles: a (before dog), The (before dog), a (before ball)

Not articles: I (pronoun), see (verb), is (verb), playing (verb)

💬 Practice Dialogue Interactive

Using Articles in Conversations

Here's how we naturally use articles when we talk. Notice how articles come before nouns!

👧 Nina:
Do you see a bird in the tree?
👦 Ricky:
Yes! It is a beautiful bird! The bird has yellow feathers.
👧 Nina:
I have an apple here. Can the bird eat it?
👦 Ricky:
Maybe! Let's put the apple on the ground for the bird!

📢 Article Counting

How many articles are in the dialogue?

a, the, a, The, an, the, the, the, the

Answer: 9 articles! Can you identify which article is used before each noun?
