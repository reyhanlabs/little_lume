📖 Introduction to Prepositions Starter

What is a Preposition?

A preposition is a word that shows LOCATION or POSITION. Prepositions tell us WHERE something is. Common prepositions are: in, on, under, above, and between.

Why Do We Need Prepositions?

Prepositions help us describe WHERE things are located. They are important for giving directions and explaining positions!

❌ Without Prepositions:
"The cat the box." (doesn't make sense)
✅ With Prepositions:
"The cat is in the box." (clear and complete)

Prepositions describe WHERE something is located.

1. In - Inside 📦

Use "in" when something is INSIDE something else. It shows that something is contained within.

Examples: in the box, in the house, in the bag, in the school, in the classroom

2. On - On Top Of 🪑

Use "on" when something is ON TOP of something. It shows that something is resting on the surface.

Examples: on the table, on the chair, on the floor, on the desk, on the roof

3. Under - Below 👇

Use "under" when something is BELOW something. It shows that something is beneath or underneath.

Examples: under the table, under the bed, under the bridge, under the tree, under the water

4. Above - Over 👆

Use "above" when something is OVER something (but not touching). It shows height and elevation.

Examples: above the clouds, above the ground, above the door, above the water, above the roof

5. Between - In the Middle 🔄

Use "between" when something is in the MIDDLE of two things. It shows something is separating two objects.

Examples: between the houses, between the trees, between the chairs, between two friends

✏️ How to Use Prepositions Practice

Position of Prepositions in Sentences

Prepositions come AFTER the verb or noun they describe. They usually come with a noun or pronoun after them.

Where Prepositions Go

Preposition + Noun:
in the box | on the table | under the chair | above the door | between the trees
Verb + Preposition + Noun:
The ball is in the box. | I sit on the chair. | The cat hides under the bed.
Complete Sentences:
The toy is in the bag. | The book is on the desk. | I sleep under the blanket.

Simple Pattern: Subject + Is + Preposition + Noun

The ball is in the box. | The cat is under the table. | The book is on the shelf.

Important Rule!

A preposition is ALWAYS followed by a noun or pronoun. Never use a preposition alone!

❌ Wrong: "The cat is in." | ✅ Correct: "The cat is in the house."

💡 Real Examples Learning

Prepositions in Everyday Life

IN Examples:
I live in a house. The pen is in the bag. The book is in the classroom.
ON Examples:
The picture is on the wall. The cup is on the table. The toy is on the shelf.
UNDER Examples:
The ball is under the bed. The cat is under the chair. The keys are under the mat.
ABOVE Examples:
The light is above the desk. The bird flies above the trees. The sign is above the door.
BETWEEN Examples:
Tom sits between Sarah and John. The park is between the school and the library. The bridge is between the two towns.

🎯 Sentences with Prepositions

4. The plane flies above the clouds.

5. I stand between my two friends.

💬 Practice Dialogue Interactive

Using Prepositions to Describe Locations

Here's how we use prepositions when we talk about where things are. Prepositions help us understand location!

👧 Luna:
Where is my toy? I cannot find it!
👦 Alex:
Is it in your room?
👧 Luna:
No, I looked in my room. Is it under the sofa?
👦 Alex:
Yes! I see it! It is under the sofa! And there is also a ball between the sofa and the chair!
👧 Luna:
Thank you! Above the sofa, on the shelf, is my favorite book!

How many prepositions are in the dialogue?

Answer: 7 prepositions! Can you identify what noun comes after each one?
