Listening Scripts

Read aloud to students · 2 scripts + 1 activity

🎙️ Script A — "Lily's Family"
Hello, friends! My name is Lily. I am six years old. 👋
Today I want to tell you about my family. I love my family so much! ❤️

First, I have a father. His name is Mr. David.
My father is tall and strong. He works as an engineer. 🔧
He builds roads and bridges. I think he is very smart!

Next, I have a mother. Her name is Mrs. Sarah.
My mother is kind and beautiful. She is a nurse. 🏥
She helps sick people get better. I am very proud of her!

I have one brother. His name is Ben. He is nine years old.
Ben is funny and smart. He loves reading books. 📚

I also have one sister. Her name is Mia. She is only two years old.
Mia is our baby. She is very little and so cute! 👶

My grandfather is Grandpa Tom. He is kind and tells great stories. 📖
My grandmother is Grandma Rose. She makes the best cookies! 🍪

We also have a pet dog. His name is Biscuit! 🐶
Biscuit is funny and playful. He is part of our family too!

My family is big and happy. We live together in our home. 🏠
I love my family very much! 😊
💡 Teacher's Tip Read Script A 2–3 times expressively. Round 1: students listen quietly. Round 2: students draw a stick-figure for each family member as they hear them. Round 3: students repeat key sentences with you. Use yellow highlighting to draw attention to family words.
🎙️ Script B — "My Small Family" (Short Version)
Hi! My name is Sam. 😊 I have a small but happy family.
There are just four people — and one very silly cat! 🐱

My father, Mr. Lee, is strong and funny. He is a cook. 🍳
My mother, Mrs. Anna, is kind and smart. She is a doctor. 🩺
I have one older sister, Zoe. She is ten and loves dancing. 💃
And our cat is called Mochi. Mochi is orange and very funny. 😄

Small family, big love! ❤️
💡 Teacher's Tip Use Script B for a second session or as a review. After listening, ask: "How many people are in Sam's family?" and "What is the cat's name?" Students can also draw Sam's family and label each member.
📋 Activity — Lily's Family Members in Order

After listening to Script A, can you list all the family members in the order Lily mentions them?

  • 1👨 Father — Mr. David (tall, strong, engineer)
  • 2👩 Mother — Mrs. Sarah (kind, beautiful, nurse)
  • 3👦 Brother — Ben, 9 years old (funny, smart, loves books)
  • 4👧 Sister — Mia, 2 years old (little, cute, the baby)
  • 5👴 Grandfather — Grandpa Tom (kind, tells great stories)
  • 6👵 Grandmother — Grandma Rose (makes the best cookies!)
  • 7🐶 Pet dog — Biscuit (funny, playful)
💡 Teacher's Tip Write each family member on a separate card and shuffle them. After listening, students arrange the cards in the correct order. This builds sequencing skills and reinforces listening comprehension.

Listening Dialogues

Real conversations about family · 2 dialogues

💬 Dialogue 1 — "Tell Me About Your Family!"
👦
Sam: Hi Lily! Can you tell me about your family?
👧
Lily: Sure, Sam! I have a big family. There are seven of us — plus our dog Biscuit!
👦
Sam: Wow, seven people! Who is in your family?
👧
Lily: My father, my mother, my brother Ben, my baby sister Mia, Grandpa Tom, Grandma Rose, and me! 😄
👦
Sam: That's a big family! What does your father do?
👧
Lily: My father is an engineer. He builds roads and bridges. He is very strong and smart! 💪
👦
Sam: Cool! What about your mother?
👧
Lily: My mother is a nurse. She is so kind and beautiful. I love her very much! ❤️
👦
Sam: Your family sounds wonderful! My family is small — just four people and our cat Mochi. 🐱
👧
Lily: A cat! How fun! Big family or small family — every family is special! 🌟
💡 Teacher's Tip Read the dialogue together — teacher as Sam, students as Lily, then swap roles. Focus on the key patterns: "What does your __ do?" and "My __ is a __." Then ask students to use those same patterns to talk about their own real family!
💬 Dialogue 2 — "Look at My Family Photo!"
👩
Teacher: Lily, whose photo is this?
👧
Lily: That is my family photo! We took it at Grandma's house last Sunday. 📸
👩
Teacher: Who is the tall man?
👧
Lily: That is my father, Mr. David. He is tall and strong. He is an engineer! 🔧
👩
Teacher: And the woman next to him — she looks so kind!
👧
Lily: That is my mother, Mrs. Sarah. She is a nurse and she is very beautiful. I love her! ❤️
👩
Teacher: Is that little baby on her lap your sister?
👧
Lily: Yes! That is baby Mia. She is two years old and so cute! 👶😍
👩
Teacher: How many people are in your family, Lily?
👧
Lily: Seven people! And one dog named Biscuit — he ran away before the photo! Ha ha! 🐶😂
👩
Teacher: Ha ha! What a wonderful family, Lily. You must feel very loved!
👧
Lily: Yes! I love my family so much! ❤️🏠
💡 Teacher's Tip Ask students to bring a family photo or draw one. After reading the dialogue, do a "show and tell" moment — each student holds up their picture and says: "This is my [family member]. His/Her name is ___. He/She is ___." This turns the listening session into a genuine, meaningful speaking activity.

Listening Exercises

Check your understanding · 3 exercise types

📝 Exercise A — Choose the Correct Answer

Listen to Script A about Lily's Family, then choose the right answer! 👇

1. What is Lily's father's name?
2. What is Lily's mother's job?
3. How old is Lily's brother, Ben?
4. What does Ben love to do?
5. What is Grandma Rose famous for?
6. What is the name of Lily's pet dog?
7. How many people are in Sam's family?
💡 Teacher's Tip Re-read both scripts before students answer. Give about 30 seconds per question. Review answers together — cheer and clap for every correct answer! 🎉 Use wrong answers as a chance to re-read the matching part of the script.
✅ Exercise B — True or False?

Based on the scripts and dialogues — is each sentence true or false?

1. Lily's father builds roads and bridges.
2. Lily's mother is a teacher.
3. Mia is two years old.
4. Sam's cat is called Biscuit.
5. Grandpa Tom loves telling great stories.
6. Lily has two brothers.
7. Sam's father is a cook.

Listening Song

My Family Song · Sing, clap & move!

🎶 "MY WONDERFUL FAMILY" — Sing Along!
👨 My father is so strong and tall, 💪
He catches me whenever I fall! 🤲
👩 My mother hugs me every day,
She chases all my fears away! 🌈

👦 My brother makes me laugh out loud, 😂
He makes our whole family so proud! 🌟
👧 My little sister, small and sweet,
She makes our family feel complete! 🍭

👴 Grandpa tells me stories of long ago,
His eyes just light up with a glow! ✨
👵 Grandma's cookies — warm and sweet,
The best thing I have ever eat! 🍪

❤️ I love my family, yes I do!
And they all love me too! 💕
Together we stand tall,
My family, best of all! 🏠🌟

♩ Sung to a gentle 4/4 beat  ·  Clap on every rhyming word! ♩
🕺 Song Actions Guide

Do these actions while singing each line!

  • 👨Father — Flex both arms like a superhero 💪
  • 👩Mother — Wrap both arms around yourself in a hug 🤗
  • 👦Brother — Make a big exaggerated laughing face 😂
  • 👧Sister — Hold hand low (she is little!) and wave sweetly 🤏
  • 👴Grandpa — Cup hands around mouth like whispering a story 📖
  • 👵Grandma — Pretend to take a big bite of a cookie 🍪😋
  • ❤️I love my family — Cross both hands over your heart ❤️
  • 🏠Best of all — Both arms up in a big V for victory! 🙌
💡 Teacher's Tip Teach the actions first without music — run through them all together, then add the song. Repeat 3 times: slow to learn → normal speed → fast and fun! Give a "Family Star" ⭐ sticker to the most expressive student. The song can also be recorded by the teacher for students to practice listening at home.
