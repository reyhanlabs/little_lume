Assessment & Testing Framework
✓ Progression Tests (Stages 3-6)
Teacher-marked assessments updated annually. Provides detailed learner performance data. Includes reporting & analysis tools.
✓ Cambridge Primary Checkpoint
Marks end of primary programme (Stage 6). International benchmark comparisons. Cambridge-marked final assessment.
✓ Formative Assessment
Ongoing classroom-based assessment. Observation of reading, writing, speaking & listening. Feedback for improvement.
✓ Reporting Standards
Unique codes for each learning objective (e.g., 5Rw3). Detailed progress reports shared with parents/guardians.
Teaching & Learning Approaches
🎯 Key Pedagogical Principles

Integrated Lessons: Each lesson ideally includes Reading, Writing, & Speaking/Listening objectives. Inquiry-Based: Promotes critical thinking, creativity, and intellectual engagement through investigation. Guided by Learning Objectives: Clear progression from Stage 1 to 6. Diverse Text Exposure: Fiction, non-fiction, poetry, playscripts across cultures & contexts. Explicit Skill Teaching: Systematic instruction in phonics, grammar, spelling, vocabulary. Collaborative Learning: Peer discussion, group work, shared reading & writing activities.

📊 Recommended Teaching Hours

Cambridge recommends 4-5 hours of English per week (approximately 120-150 hours per stage over a full year). This ensures adequate time for integrated skill development, consolidation of learning, and assessment activities.
