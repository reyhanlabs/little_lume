🌟 What is a Pronoun? Core Concept

Definition of a Pronoun

A pronoun is a word used to replace a noun (the name of a person, animal, or thing) so we don't have to repeat it over and over.

Example: Instead of saying "Budi went to school. Budi carried Budi's bag." — we say "Budi went to school. He carried his bag."

Without Pronouns vs. With Pronouns

🙋 Subject Pronouns The Doer

What is a Subject Pronoun?

A subject pronoun is the pronoun that acts as the subject (the doer) of a sentence. It usually comes at the beginning of the sentence, before the verb.

Pattern: Subject Pronoun + Verb + ...

Full Reference Table

Subject Pronouns & Example Sentences

PronounRefers toExample Sentence
IyourselfI go to school every day.→ You are talking about yourself.
Youthe person spoken toYou are my best friend.→ Talking to one person.
Heone male personHe plays football very well.→ Replaces a boy's or man's name.
Sheone female personShe likes drawing pictures.→ Replaces a girl's or woman's name.
Itan animal or thingIt is a big dog.→ Replaces a thing or animal.
Weyourself + other(s)We study English together.→ Includes yourself in the group.
Theymore than one personThey live in Jakarta.→ Replaces a group of people.
Sentence Builder

🔨 How to Build a Sentence

She+reads+a book → She reads a book.
They+play+in the park → They play in the park.
We+eat+rice → We eat rice.
He+watches+TV → He watches TV.
⚠️
Important! The pronoun "I" is always written with a capital letter, even in the middle of a sentence.
✅ My mom and I go shopping.
❌ My mom and i go shopping.
🎯 Object Pronouns The Receiver

What is an Object Pronoun?

An object pronoun is the pronoun that receives the action in a sentence. It usually comes after a verb or after a preposition (to, for, with, etc.).

Pattern: Subject + Verb + Object Pronoun

🦊
Easy trick: Object pronouns always come after the verb.
"I love her" ✅  |  "Her love I" ❌

They also appear after prepositions: to, for, with, about, at, from
Example: "This gift is for him."
💼 Possessive Pronouns Ownership

What is a Possessive Pronoun?

Possessive pronouns show who owns something. There are two forms:
🔹 Possessive Adjective – placed before a noun (my bag, his book)
🔸 Possessive Pronoun – stands alone, without a noun (mine, his, hers)

Full Reference Table

Possessive Adjectives & Possessive Pronouns

SubjectPoss. AdjectivePoss. PronounExample
I my mine This is my book. → This book is mine.→ The book belongs to me.
You your yours Is this your pen? → Is this pen yours?→ Does the pen belong to you?
He his his That is his bag. → That bag is his.→ The bag belongs to him.
She her hers Her cat is cute. → The cat is hers.→ The cat belongs to her.
It its — The dog wags its tail.→ No standalone form; always used with a noun.
We our ours This is our school. → The school is ours.→ The school belongs to us.
They their theirs Their house is big. → The house is theirs.→ The house belongs to them.
Comparison

Possessive Adjective vs. Possessive Pronoun

🔹 Possessive Adjective
(+ a noun after it)

  • This is my bag.
  • I love her smile.
  • They lost their key.
  • We clean our room.

🔸 Possessive Pronoun
(stands alone, no noun)

  • This bag is mine.
  • The smile is hers.
  • The key is theirs.
  • The room is ours.
⚠️
Don't mix these up!
🐾 Its (ownership by an animal/thing) is DIFFERENT from It's (= It is).
✅ The cat licks its paw. → ownership — belongs to the cat.
✅ It's a cute cat. → It is a cute cat.
💬 Dialogue Practice Real Conversations
Dialogue 1 – Talking About a Friend

Who is That? 🤔

👧
Nisa
Hey Dani! Who is that boy over there? He looks familiar.
→ "He" = subject pronoun replacing the boy's name.
👦
Dani
Oh, he is Reza. He is my neighbor. His house is near our school.
→ "my" & "his" = possessive adjectives. "our" = possessive adjective (plural).
👧
Nisa
Really? Does he know us?
→ "us" = object pronoun replacing "Nisa and Dani".
👦
Dani
Yes! He knows you too. He told me that you are his classmate in tutor class!
→ "you" = object pronoun. "me" = object pronoun. "his" = possessive adjective.
👧
Nisa
Wow! Let's invite him to play with us!
→ "him" & "us" = object pronouns.
Dialogue 2 – Whose Pencil Case is This?

Whose Is It? 🎒

👩‍🏫
Teacher
Class, I found a pencil case. Is this yours, Budi?
→ "yours" = possessive pronoun (stands alone, no noun after it).
👦
Budi
No, Ma'am. That is not mine. My pencil case is blue.
→ "mine" = possessive pronoun. "My" = possessive adjective (+ noun).
👩‍🏫
Teacher
How about you, Sari? Could it be hers? (Wulan's)
→ "hers" = possessive pronoun replacing "Wulan's pencil case".
👧
Sari
Oh yes! That is hers! She told me she lost her pencil case yesterday!
→ "She" = subject pronoun. "me" = object pronoun. "her" = possessive adjective.
🦊
Try this practice! Read both dialogues above and underline every pronoun you find. Then sort them into three groups: Subject Pronoun, Object Pronoun, and Possessive. You'll find plenty of real examples! 🎉
