🎯 What Are Articles? Foundation

Have you ever noticed small words like "a," "an," and "the" before nouns? These special words are called articles, and they are very important in English!

Why Do Articles Matter?

Articles are tiny words, but they help us communicate clearly. They tell us if something is general or specific, and whether we're talking about one thing or something we've already mentioned.

Articles are the most commonly used words in English! We use them many times every day when we speak and write.

💡 Types of Articles Structure

The Three Articles

There are three articles in English, and each one has a special use:

A
|
AN
|
THE
= All Articles
Each article has a specific purpose and rule

A - Consonant Sounds

Used before consonant SOUNDS (not letters!). Use when talking about something for the first time.

Examples: a boy, a dog, a house, a car, a pencil

An - Vowel Sounds

Used before vowel SOUNDS (a, e, i, o, u). Use when talking about something for the first time.

Examples: an apple, an elephant, an ice cream, an umbrella, an orange

The - Specific Things

The definite article. Use when talking about something specific - something we already know about or something that is clear which one we mean.

Examples: the teacher, the sun, the classroom, the book

✅ A & An (Indefinite)

  • a cat - any cat
  • an apple - any apple
  • First mention
  • General/unknown

✅ The (Definite)

  • the cat - a specific cat
  • the apple - we know which one
  • Already mentioned
  • Specific/known

✏️ How to Use Articles Practice

Sound Matters, Not Letters!

Remember: We use A or AN based on SOUNDS, not the first letter!

A or An - Sound Rules

A (consonant sounds):
a boy, a dog, a book, a pencil
An (vowel sounds):
an apple, an elephant, an orange, an umbrella
Special cases:
a university (u sounds like "yu"), an hour (h is silent)

First Mention vs Second Mention

Notice how articles change:

First time:
I saw a cat in the street.
Second time:
The cat was very cute.
First time:
My friend has a dog.
Second time:
The dog likes to play.

When to Use The

Use "the" for:

Specific/known things:
The sun is bright. (There is only one sun)
Already mentioned:
I have a cat. The cat is orange.
Clear which one:
I like the teacher. (My teacher, the one we know)

📚 Real Examples Sentences

Using A

Sentence:
I have a pencil.
Sentence:
She is a good student.
Sentence:
This is a beautiful flower.

Using An

Sentence:
I eat an apple every day.
Sentence:
He is an excellent teacher.
Sentence:
This is an interesting book.

Using The

Sentence:
The sun rises in the morning.
Sentence:
I like the teacher because she is kind.
Sentence:
The book is on the table.

💬 Conversation Speaking

Listen to how Siti and Ahmad use articles when they talk about their pets:

Siti:
"Ahmad, do you have a pet?"
Ahmad:
"Yes! I have a dog and an cat. The dog is brown, and the cat is orange."
Siti:
"How nice! I also have a pet. It's an aquarium with fish."
Ahmad:
"Cool! What's the name of your aquarium?"
Siti:
"I call it 'Rainbow Home' because the fish have many colors. They are beautiful!"
Ahmad:
"That's a great name! I would like to see the aquarium sometime."
