🎯 What Are Nouns? Foundation

Welcome to the world of Nouns! Nouns are one of the most important parts of English grammar. Every day, we use nouns when we talk, read, and write.

What is a Noun?

A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea. Nouns are everywhere! When you look around your classroom, you can see many nouns - your desk, your chair, your pencil, and your friends!

The word "noun" comes from the Latin word "nomen," which means "name." So nouns are literally naming words!

💡 Types of Nouns Structure

Two Main Types of Nouns

Every noun is either common or proper

🔤 Common Nouns

These name general people, places, or things. They start with a lowercase letter.

Examples: boy, girl, dog, cat, school, park, book, toy

🔤 Proper Nouns

These name specific people, places, or things. They ALWAYS start with a capital letter.

Examples: Ali, Sarah, Indonesia, Jakarta, Monday, December

📊 Singular vs Plural

Singular Noun: One person, place, or thing (cat, book, pencil)
Plural Noun: More than one (cats, books, pencils)

🔢 Countable vs Uncountable

Countable: We can count them (apple, book, pencil)
Uncountable: We cannot count as single items (water, milk, sand)

✏️ How to Use Nouns Practice

Where Nouns Appear in Sentences

Nouns can be the subject (doing action) or object (receiving action) in a sentence.

Nouns as Subject

The person or thing doing something:

Subject Position:
Aldi plays football.
Subject Position:
The cat drinks milk.
Subject Position:
My teacher reads books.

Nouns as Object

The person or thing receiving the action:

Object Position:
I love chocolate.
Object Position:
She reads storybooks.
Object Position:
We visit grandma on Sunday.

Plural Rules

How to make nouns plural:

Add -s:
cat → cats, dog → dogs
Add -es (s, x, z, ch, sh):
box → boxes, church → churches
Special words:
child → children, man → men
