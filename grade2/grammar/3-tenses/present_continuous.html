🎯 What is Present Continuous? Foundation

When we say "I am playing football RIGHT NOW" or "She is reading a book," we use the Present Continuous tense. It shows actions happening at this very moment!

What is Present Continuous Tense?

The Present Continuous tense describes actions that are happening RIGHT NOW, at this moment!

💡 Present Continuous Structure Grammar

Formula: Is/Are + Verb-ing

We use "am/is/are" + the verb with -ing at the end.

Am/Is/Are
+
Verb + ing
=Present Continuous

I am + verb-ing

I am playing, I am reading, I am eating

He/She/It is + verb-ing

He is playing, She is reading, It is running

You/We/They are + verb-ing

You are playing, We are reading, They are running

✏️ How to Use Present Continuous Practice

Right Now - This Moment!

Use it for actions happening NOW:

Example:
I am writing my homework right now.
Example:
He is playing basketball at the park.
Example:
They are watching TV.

-ing Rules

Normal:
play → playing, read → reading
Drop -e:
make → making, ride → riding
Double letter:
run → running, sit → sitting

📚 Real Examples Sentences

Actions Happening Now

Sentence:
The cat is sleeping on the sofa.
Sentence:
My mother is cooking lunch.
Sentence:
The children are playing in the garden.

Present Simple vs Present Continuous

Present Simple

  • I play football (usually)
  • She reads books (normally)
  • They eat lunch (routine)

Present Continuous

  • I am playing football (now!)
  • She is reading (right now!)
  • They are eating (at this moment!)
