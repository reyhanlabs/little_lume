🎯 What is Present Simple? Foundation

When we say "I play football every day" or "She goes to school," we use the Present Simple tense. It tells us about things that happen regularly or are always true!

What is Present Simple Tense?

The Present Simple tense describes actions that happen regularly, every day, or are always true. It's the most basic and common tense we use!

We use Present Simple for habits, daily routines, and facts that are always true. It's what we use to talk about normal life!

💡 Present Simple Structure Grammar

Basic Structure

Present Simple is simple! For most subjects, we just use the base form of the verb.

Subject
+
Base Verb
=Present Simple
I play, You play, They play

Most Subjects (I, You, We, They)

For these subjects, use the basic form of the verb:

Formula: I/You/We/They + base verb
Examples: I play, You eat, We run, They walk

He/She/It (Third Person Singular)

For He/She/It, add -s or -es to the verb:

Formula: He/She/It + verb + -s/-es
Examples: He plays, She eats, It runs

No -s/-es

  • I play
  • You eat
  • We run
  • They walk

Add -s/-es

  • He plays
  • She eats
  • It runs
  • Ani walks

✏️ When to Use Present Simple Practice

Three Main Uses

We use Present Simple in three main situations:

1. Regular Habits & Routines

Things we do every day or regularly:

Example:
I brush my teeth every morning.
Example:
She goes to school at 7 AM.
Example:
They play football after school.

2. General Truths & Facts

Things that are always true:

Example:
Cats like milk.
Example:
The sun rises in the east.
Example:
Water boils at 100 degrees.

3. What You Like or Do

Your likes, dislikes, and abilities:

Example:
I like chocolate ice cream.
Example:
He can swim very fast.
Example:
We don't like vegetables.

💬 Conversation Speaking

Listen to how Tara and Ravi talk about their daily routines:

Tara:
"What time do you wake up every day?"
Ravi:
"I wake up at 6 AM. Then I exercise for 30 minutes."
Tara:
"That's great! I don't exercise much. I go to school at 7 AM."
Ravi:
"What do you do after school? Do you play any sports?"
Tara:
"Yes! I play basketball. I really like it. What about you?"
Ravi:
"I prefer swimming. I go swimming three times a week."
