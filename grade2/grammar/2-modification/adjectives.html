🎯 What Are Adjectives? Foundation

When we say "The flower is red," the word red describes the flower. Words that describe nouns are called adjectives! They make our writing more interesting and colorful.

What is an Adjective?

An adjective is a word that describes or modifies a noun. It tells us more about what something looks like, feels like, or is like.

Without adjectives, our sentences would be boring! Adjectives add color, emotion, and detail to our words.

✏️ How to Use Adjectives Practice

Adjectives Come Before or After Nouns

Adjectives usually come before the noun, but sometimes they come after with linking verbs.

Adjectives Before the Noun

Most adjectives come right before the noun:

Example:
A red ball is in the garden.
Example:
The big dog is sleeping.
Example:
She has long black hair.

Adjectives After "To Be"

After linking verbs like "is," "are," "am," the adjective comes after:

Example:
The flower is beautiful.
Example:
The ice cream is cold.
Example:
I am happy.

Multiple Adjectives

We can use more than one adjective to describe something:

Example:
A big red ball
Example:
The small cute kitten
Example:
Fresh hot pizza is delicious.
