🎯 What Are Prepositions? Foundation

When we say "The book is on the table" or "I am in the classroom," words like on and in tell us WHERE something is. These are called prepositions!

What is a Preposition?

A preposition is a word that shows the relationship between nouns or pronouns and other words in a sentence. Most prepositions show position or location - where something is!

Prepositions are small words, but they're very important for showing where things are and how they relate to each other.

=Location Prepositions
Words that show where things are

📍 In - Inside Something

Use "in" for something inside a container or place:

Examples: in the house, in the box, in the classroom, in the bag

📍 On - Touching or On Top

Use "on" for something touching or resting on top of something:

Examples: on the table, on the wall, on the floor, on the chair

📍 At - A Specific Place

Use "at" for a specific point or place:

Examples: at school, at home, at the park, at the door

📍 Under - Below Something

Use "under" for something below or beneath:

Examples: under the table, under the bed, under the tree

📍 Between - In the Middle

Use "between" for something in the middle of two things:

Examples: between the trees, between two people, between the buildings

✏️ How to Use Prepositions Practice

Prepositions in Sentences

Prepositions usually come before a noun or pronoun.

Position Prepositions

These show where something is located:

Example:
The cat is under the bed.
Example:
My pencil is on the desk.
Example:
I am in the classroom.

Common Preposition Combinations

These phrases are used very often:

At:
at school, at home, at the park, at work
In:
in the house, in the car, in the room, in the box
On:
on the floor, on the wall, on the table, on the sofa

Multiple Prepositions

Sometimes we use more than one preposition to be very specific:

Example:
The book is on top of the desk.
Example:
He is next to the door.
Example:
We stand in front of the classroom.

Prepositions Show Relationships

Same word, different meaning:
In the box / On the box / Under the box
Each preposition changes the meaning!
The toy is in/on/under the box changes where the toy is!

💬 Conversation Speaking

Listen to how Mita and Raka use prepositions to describe locations:

Mita:
"Raka! Where is my book?"
Raka:
"I think it's on your desk. Or maybe under the table?"
Mita:
"Let me look. No, it's not there. Where else could it be? Maybe in my bag?"
Raka:
"Check at the library! Didn't you take a book there?"
Mita:
"Oh yes! I left it between the other books on the shelf!"
Raka:
"Found it! It's right there in the library. Go get it!"
