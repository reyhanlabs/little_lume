🎯 What Are Verbs? Foundation

Every day, you do many things - you run, jump, eat, sleep, and play. These action words are called verbs! Verbs are the words that tell us what someone is doing.

What is a Verb?

A verb is a word that shows an action or a state of being. Verbs answer the question "What is someone doing?" or "What is happening?"

Verbs are everywhere in our language! Without verbs, we couldn't describe actions or movement. They give energy to our sentences!

💡 Types of Verbs Structure

Two Kinds of Verbs

Verbs can be divided into two main groups:

Different verbs, different purposes

🏃 Action Verbs

These show what someone or something is doing. You can usually act them out!

Examples: run, jump, play, eat, sing, dance, write, draw, kick, throw

🔗 Linking Verbs

These connect the subject to a description. The most common linking verb is "be" (am, is, are).

Examples: am, is, are, was, were, feel, look, smell, taste, sound

✅ Action Verbs

  • run - moving fast
  • jump - moving upward
  • eat - taking food
  • sing - making music
  • write - making words

✅ Linking Verbs

  • am - I am happy
  • is - She is smart
  • are - They are friends
  • feel - I feel tired
  • look - It looks blue

✏️ How to Use Verbs Practice

Verbs in Sentences

Every sentence needs a verb! The verb is the action or state of being in a sentence.

Present Tense - What's Happening Now

Use simple verbs to show actions happening now:

Example:
I play football.
Example:
She reads a book.
Example:
They walk to school.

Past Tense - What Happened Before

Add -ed to most verbs to show past actions:

Example:
I played yesterday.
Example:
She walked home.
Special verbs:
I went, she ate, he saw

Verbs Change with Subjects

Some verbs change depending on who is doing the action:

With He/She/It:
He plays (add -s)
With I/You/We/They:
I play (no -s)

💬 Conversation Speaking

Listen to how Rina and Fandi use verbs when they talk about their day:

Rina:
"Hi Fandi! What did you do after school today?"
Fandi:
"I played football with my friends. We had so much fun!"
Rina:
"That sounds fun! I went to the park and rode my bicycle. My legs feel tired now."
Fandi:
"Let's rest for a while. Do you want to eat some snacks?"
Rina:
"Great idea! I ate breakfast this morning, but I'm hungry now. Let's go to the canteen."
