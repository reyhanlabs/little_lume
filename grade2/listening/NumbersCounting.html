📌 Lesson 1 – Numbers 1-10

👂 Sound Patterns in Numbers
  • ONE, TWO, THREE, FOUR, FIVE - Each has different sound
  • SIX, SEVEN, EIGHT, NINE - All end in similar sounds
  • TEN - Special number, very common
📚 Numbers in Use

Examples of using numbers 1-10:
"I have three books." (you have 3)
"She is five years old." (age 5)
"The answer is eight." (answer = 8)
"Can you count to ten?" (count: 1,2,3...10)

👂 Practice – Listen & Identify Numbers
  1. Teacher says "three". You show 3 fingers.
  2. Listen to a number and write it: one, five, seven, ten, etc.
  3. Count from 1-10 out loud after your teacher.
  4. Your teacher says a number, you clap that many times. (e.g., "five" → you clap 5 times)

👂 Important Teen Numbers
  • ELEVEN (11) - Different! Not "one-teen"
  • TWELVE (12) - Different! Not "two-teen"
  • THIRTEEN to NINETEEN - Have "-teen" sound
  • TWENTY (20) - New word, not "two-teen"
📚 How Old Are You?

"How old are you?" - Question asking your age
Answers: "I am five." / "I am twelve." / "I am twenty."

"How old is your sister?"
"She is fifteen."

👂 Practice – Teen Numbers
  1. Listen and say numbers 11-20 after your teacher.
  2. Your teacher says a teen number (11-20). Write it: thirteen, eighteen, fourteen, etc.
  3. Practice your age: "I am _____ years old." (Use numbers 5-10 or 11-20)
  4. Tell about your friends: "My friend is ____ years old." (Use a number)
📌 Lesson 3 – Counting & Quantities
📊 How Many?
When you see objects, you count them and say "how many". Listen for the word "how many" - it's a question asking about quantity. Then you answer with a number and the name of the objects.
👂 Listen to Quantity Questions & Answers

Question: "How many apples?" →
Answer: "Five apples." (5 🍎)

Question: "How many students?" →
Answer: "Twelve students." (12 👧👦)

Question: "How many books do you have?" →
Answer: "I have three books." (3 📖)

📚 Common Things to Count
  • Apples 🍎 - "How many apples?" / "Three apples"
  • Books 📖 - "How many books?" / "Eight books"
  • Friends 👫 - "How many friends?" / "Seven friends"
  • Pencils ✏️ - "How many pencils?" / "Ten pencils"
  • Fingers 👆 - "How many fingers?" / "Ten fingers"
🎧 Sample Counting Dialogue

Teacher: "Count the chairs in our classroom."
Student: "One, two, three... fifteen. There are fifteen chairs."

Teacher: "How many girls in our class?"
Student: "There are eight girls."

👂 Practice – Counting Objects
  1. Teacher asks "How many ___?" (apples/books/friends). You count and answer.
  2. Look at classroom objects. Teacher points, you count and say the number.
  3. Listen to a counting story: "I have two cats. I have one dog. How many pets?" Answer: three.
  4. Practice your own: "I have __ pencils / __ books / __ friends." (Use a number)
📌 Lesson 4 – Age & Numbers in Real Life
🎂 Numbers Every Day
Numbers are used every day for many things: to tell age, to write dates, to show time, to count money, and much more. In this lesson, you practice listening to numbers used in real life situations.
👂 Numbers in Daily Life
  • Age: "I am six years old." / "My brother is ten."
  • Classroom: "We have twenty students in our class."
  • School Subjects: "This book has fifteen chapters."
  • Sports & Games: "I scored eight points!" / "The score is four to one."
  • Home: "I have two sisters." / "My house number is thirteen."
💬 Real Conversations with Numbers

Conversation 1 – About Age:
Friend: "How old are you?"
You: "I am seven years old!"
Friend: "I am seven too! We are the same age!"

Conversation 2 – About School:
Teacher: "How many students are here today?"
Student: "Eighteen students are here, teacher."
Teacher: "Good! Two students are absent."

Conversation 3 – About Family:
Grandmother: "How many cousins do you have?"
Child: "I have five cousins!"

👂 Practice – Real-Life Numbers
  1. When someone asks "How old are you?", answer with your real age in English.
  2. Look around: Count students in class, pencils, books, etc. Say the total number.
  3. Listen to your teacher ask number questions about your school and family. Answer with numbers.
  4. Share: "I have ___ friends / ___ toys / ___ pets." (Use a number)
📚 Vocabulary Bank
Number Words Usage / Example
One, two, three...ten Basic numbers - "I have three books"
Eleven, twelve...twenty Teen numbers - "I am twelve years old"
How many? Question asking about quantity
Count To say numbers in order - "Count from one to ten"
Total The final number when you add - "The total is five"
Age How old someone is - "I am seven years old"
Number A symbol or word for quantity (1, 2, one, two)
Zero The number 0 - nothing - "Zero apples"
💡 Tips for Learning Numbers
👂
Repeat Out Loud Say numbers 1-20 every day. The more you say them, the better you remember!
🖐️
Use Your Fingers Count on your fingers while saying numbers. This helps you remember.
🎧
Listen Many Times Listen to English songs about numbers. YouTube has many counting songs!
🔢
Count Everything Count things around you: pencils, students, apples. Say numbers in English!
🎲
Play Games Play number games with friends. Clap, draw, or point when you hear numbers.
📝
Write Numbers Write number words: one, two, three... and practice saying them.
