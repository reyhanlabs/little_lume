📌 Lesson 1 – Morning Greetings
👋 Hello! Good Morning!
When you see a friend or teacher in the morning, you should say hello or good morning. Greetings show that you are happy to see them. People will often answer your greeting with a friendly greeting back!
👂 Listen to These Greetings

Person A: "Hello!" → Person B: "Hello!"

Person A: "Good morning!" → Person B: "Good morning!"

Person A: "Hi, how are you?" → Person B: "I'm fine, thank you!"

Person A: "Hello! Nice to meet you!" → Person B: "Nice to meet you too!"

🎧 Common Morning Greetings for Grade 2
  • "Hello!" (most common, use anytime)
  • "Good morning!" (use in the morning)
  • "Hi!" (short, friendly)
  • "Hey!" (friendly, informal)
  • "Good morning! How are you?" (polite & friendly)
💬 Sample Greeting Conversations

Conversation 1:
Teacher: "Good morning, class!"
Students: "Good morning, teacher!"

Conversation 2:
Rina: "Hello, Budi!"
Budi: "Hi, Rina! How are you?"
Rina: "I'm fine, thank you!"

👂 Practice – Listen & Respond
  1. Your teacher says "Good morning!" What do you say? (Good morning!)
  2. You hear "Hello, how are you?" How do you answer?
  3. Listen to your teacher say different greetings and repeat each one.
  4. Practice with a classmate: Take turns saying greetings and responding.
📌 Lesson 2 – How Are You? (Feelings)
😊 Asking About Feelings
After you say hello, friends often ask "How are you?" This question asks about how you feel. In Grade 2, you learn to listen to this question and give simple answers about your feelings: fine, happy, sad, tired, excited!
👂 Listen to the Question & Answers

Question: "How are you?"

Possible Answers:
✓ "I'm fine, thank you!" (good, healthy)
✓ "I'm happy!" (full of joy)
✓ "I'm sad." (not happy)
✓ "I'm tired." (need sleep)
✓ "I'm excited!" (very happy about something)

🎧 Words About Feelings
FeelingExample
happy 😊I'm happy! (You smile)
sad 😢I'm sad. (You don't smile)
fine ✓I'm fine! (Everything is okay)
tired 😴I'm tired. (Need sleep)
excited 🎉I'm excited! (Very, very happy)
💬 Conversation Examples

Example 1:
Friend: "How are you today?"
You: "I'm happy! I have a birthday cake!"

Example 2:
Mom: "How are you?"
You: "I'm tired. I want to sleep."

👂 Practice – Feeling Words
  1. Teacher asks "How are you?" Answer with a feeling word: happy, sad, fine, tired, or excited.
  2. Listen to sentences and identify the feeling:
    • "I want to sleep." → You are _____ (tired)
    • "I have a new toy!" → You are _____ (happy)
  3. Your teacher says "I'm sad." Can you ask why? ("Why are you sad?")
  4. Practice with a friend: Ask "How are you?" and give different feeling answers each time.
📌 Lesson 3 – Goodbye & Evening Greetings
🌙 Saying Goodbye
When you finish playing or learning, you say goodbye to your friends. Goodbye means you will see them again later. There are different ways to say goodbye: Goodbye, Bye, See you soon, Goodnight (at bedtime).
👂 Listen to Goodbyes

Friend 1: "Goodbye!" → Friend 2: "Goodbye!"

Friend 1: "Bye, see you tomorrow!" → Friend 2: "See you!"

Parent: "Goodnight, sweet dreams!" → Child: "Goodnight!"

Friend 1: "See you later!" → Friend 2: "See you!"

🎧 Different Ways to Say Goodbye
  • "Goodbye!" - formal, polite
  • "Bye!" - short, friendly
  • "See you later!" - you will see them soon
  • "See you tomorrow!" - you will see them the next day
  • "See you soon!" - you will see them very soon
  • "Goodnight!" - when going to sleep
💬 Evening Goodbye Conversations

After School:
Teacher: "Goodbye, children! Have a nice day!"
Students: "Goodbye, teacher!"

Bedtime at Home:
Mom: "Goodnight, sweet dreams!"
Child: "Goodnight, Mom!"

👂 Practice – Say Goodbye
  1. Your teacher says "Goodbye, class!" What do you answer? (Goodbye!)
  2. You hear "See you tomorrow!" What can you say back?
  3. Listen to different goodbyes and repeat: Bye! / See you! / Goodnight!
  4. Say goodbye to your teacher with feeling: "Goodbye, teacher! Thank you!"
📌 Lesson 4 – Short Friendly Dialogues
💬 Listening to Conversations
A dialogue is a conversation between two people. In this lesson, you listen to short, simple dialogues using greetings and responses. This helps you understand how English speakers talk to each other!
👂 Full Dialogue Examples

Dialogue 1: Morning at School
Ani: "Good morning, Rudi!"
Rudi: "Good morning, Ani! How are you?"
Ani: "I'm fine, thank you! How are you?"
Rudi: "I'm happy! I have a new book!"
Ani: "That's nice!"

Dialogue 2: Meeting a New Friend
Tina: "Hi! I'm Tina."
Sani: "Hello! I'm Sani. Nice to meet you!"
Tina: "Nice to meet you too! How are you?"
Sani: "I'm fine! Do you want to play?"
Tina: "Yes! Let's play together!"

Dialogue 3: End of School Day
Teacher: "Goodbye, children! See you tomorrow!"
Students: "Goodbye, teacher! Thank you!"
Budi: "Bye, Rina! See you tomorrow!"
Rina: "See you, Budi!"

📚 Key Words in Greetings & Farewells
  • Hello / Hi - to say hi to someone
  • Good morning - greeting in the morning
  • How are you? - ask about someone's feelings
  • I'm fine / happy / sad / tired - answer about feelings
  • Thank you - say thanks
  • Goodbye / Bye - say bye to someone
  • See you later / tomorrow - you will see them again
👂 Practice – Listen to Dialogues
  1. Listen to your teacher read a dialogue. Repeat the same dialogue with a classmate.
  2. Your teacher says the first part. You say the second part:
    • Teacher: "Good morning!" → You: "Good morning!"
    • Teacher: "How are you?" → You: "I'm ___" (fine/happy/sad/tired)
  3. Create your own short dialogue with a friend (3-4 lines).
  4. Listen to a dialogue and answer: "How many people are talking?" "What are they saying?"

💡 Tips for Learning Greetings
😊
Be Friendly When you greet someone, smile and look happy. This shows you are kind and friendly.
👂
Listen Carefully Pay attention to how your teacher or friends say greetings. Copy the same tone and feeling.
🔊
Speak Clearly Speak loud enough. Say each word slowly so people can understand you.
👀
Look at People When you say hello or goodbye, look at the other person's face. This is polite!
🎭
Use Your Voice Use happy, friendly voice for hellos. Use warm, kind voice for goodbyes.
💪
Practice Always Say greetings every day! Morning hello, goodbye at night, and with your friends!

  • I can listen to a simple greeting dialogue and understand what is happening.
