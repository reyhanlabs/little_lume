📌 Lesson 1 – Listening to the Alphabet
🔤 The English Alphabet Sounds
The English alphabet has 26 letters. Each letter has a name and a sound. When you learn English, you must practice listening to how each letter sounds.
🎧 Listen to These Letters

A - sounds like "ay" (apple, ant, apple pie)
B - sounds like "bee" (ball, bat, book)
C - sounds like "cee" or "kuh" (cat, car, cup)
E - sounds like "ee" (egg, elephant, eleven)
I - sounds like "eye" (ice, insect, igloo)

👂 Vowel Sounds (Easy to Remember)
  • A - "ay" - Apple 🍎
  • E - "eh" - Elephant 🐘
  • I - "eye" - Ice cream 🍦
  • O - "oh" - Orange 🍊
  • U - "oo" - Umbrella ☂️
👂 Practice – Identify the Letter Sound
  1. Your teacher says "A, A, A". What letter is it? (Answer: A)
  2. Listen: "B, B, B". Write the letter you hear.
  3. I say "E, E, E". Can you say it back?
  4. Clap your hands for each vowel sound you hear: A, E, I, O, U.
📌 Lesson 2 – Listening to Word Sounds
🎵 Phonics & Beginning Sounds
Phonics is learning how letters make sounds. When letters come together, they make words. Listen carefully to the beginning sound (first sound) of each word.
🎧 Listen to Beginning Sounds
  • /b/ - ball, boy, book, bed - All start with the "b" sound
  • /c/ - cat, car, cake, cap - All start with the "c" sound
  • /d/ - dog, day, desk, door - All start with the "d" sound
  • /p/ - pen, pig, play, park - All start with the "p" sound
  • /s/ - sun, sit, smile, sit - All start with the "s" sound
👂 Sound Matching Pairs
WordBeginning Sound
cat/k/ sound
dog/d/ sound
sit/s/ sound
pig/p/ sound
👂 Practice – Listening to Word Sounds
  1. I say "cat". What sound is at the beginning? (/k/ sound)
  2. Listen to: dog, desk, door. What sound do they all have?
  3. Which word starts with the /p/ sound? (pig, sit, book) - Answer: pig
  4. Clap twice when you hear a word that starts with /s/.
📌 Lesson 3 – Listening to Simple Words
🎧 Common Classroom Words
In Grade 2, you learn to listen to simple, everyday words. These are words your teacher uses in class every day. Listen carefully so you understand what your teacher is asking you to do.
🎧 Listen to These Classroom Words
  • sit down - Please sit down now
  • stand up - Stand up, please
  • open your book - Open your book
  • listen to me - Listen to me, please
  • raise your hand - Raise your hand if you know
  • be quiet - Be quiet, please
📚 Simple Object Words for Grade 2
  • pen ✏️, pencil ✏️, book 📖, bag 🎒
  • apple 🍎, desk 📚, chair 🪑, door 🚪
  • sun ☀️, moon 🌙, star ⭐, tree 🌳
👂 Practice – Understanding Classroom Words
  1. When I say "sit down", you sit. When I say "stand up", you stand. Let's practice!
  2. Listen: I say different words. Circle the words you hear: (pen, apple, chair, tree)
  3. Match the word to the action:
    • "open your book" → You open a book
    • "raise your hand" → You put up your hand
    • "be quiet" → You stop talking
📌 Lesson 4 – Following Simple Instructions
📢 Listen and Do!
Instructions tell you what to do. When your teacher gives an instruction in English, you must listen carefully and understand what to do. Simple instructions use action words like: sit, stand, open, close, point, touch, clap.
📢 Common Instructions for Grade 2
  • "Sit down" - Move to a seat and sit
  • "Stand up" - Get up from your seat
  • "Open your book" - Take your book and open it
  • "Close your eyes" - Keep your eyes shut
  • "Point to the door" - Use your finger to show the door
  • "Clap your hands" - Hit your hands together
  • "Touch your nose" - Put your finger on your nose
👂 Sample Instruction Sequences

Instruction 1: "Sit down and open your book to page 5."
What you do: Find a seat, sit, open your book, turn to page 5

Instruction 2: "Stand up, close your eyes, and point to the window."
What you do: Stand up, close your eyes, point to where the window is

👂 Practice – Follow the Instructions!
  1. Simple: Your teacher says "clap your hands". You clap.
  2. Two parts: "Stand up and touch your head."
  3. Three parts: "Sit down, open your book, and point to a picture."
  4. Listen to your teacher give 5 different instructions. Do each one!

💡 Tips for Good Listening
👂
Focus Look at the speaker and listen carefully. Close your eyes if it helps you hear better.
🤐
Be Quiet No talking when someone is speaking. Your friends need quiet to listen too.
🎧
Repeat Say the words you hear out loud. This helps you remember and practice.
🤔
Think Think about what you hear. What word is it? What sound? What should you do?
✋
Raise Your Hand If you don't understand, raise your hand. Ask your teacher to explain.
💪
Practice Every Day Listen to English songs, videos, and stories. This helps you get better!
✅ Self-Check – Can You Do This?
  • I can identify the sounds of English letters (A-Z).
  • I understand the beginning sounds (phonics) of simple words.
  • I can listen to and understand simple classroom vocabulary.
  • I can follow simple one-part instructions (sit down, stand up).
  • I can follow two-part instructions (sit down + open your book).
  • I can listen carefully without talking when others are speaking.
  • I can repeat words and sounds I hear.
