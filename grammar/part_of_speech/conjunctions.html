What is a Conjunction? 🤔

Conjunction (word connector) is a magic word that connects two words, two ideas, or two sentences into one! Conjunctions are like word glue 🧩 — without them, our sentences will be choppy and boring.

😴

Without Conjunction

"I like cats. I like dogs."
"It rained. We stayed home."

Sounds stiff & choppy ✂️

🤩

With Conjunction

"I like cats and dogs."
"It rained, so we stayed home."

Flows & reads nicely! 🌊

CAUSE & EFFECT

"Because" vs "So" — Don't Mix Them Up! 🔄

These two words often confuse students. Let's see the arrow direction so you never mix them up again!

BECAUSE = SINCE

Explains the REASON (cause), usually mentioned after the effect

Effect ✅
⬅ because
Reason 💡

"I stayed home because it rained."

(Effect: stayed home) ⬅ (Reason: rained)

SO = THEREFORE / THUS

Explains the REASON first, then the effect

Reason 💡
so ➡
Effect ✅

"It rained, so I stayed home."

(Reason: rained) ➡ (Effect: stayed home)
