📌 Lesson 1 – What Is an Adjective?
🔍 Describing Words
An adjective is a word that gives more information about a noun (a person, animal, place, or thing). It answers questions like: What colour? What size? How does it feel? What is it like?
✨ Example Sentences
  • The big elephant is walking slowly. (big describes the elephant)
  • I have a red bag. (red describes the bag)
  • This soup is hot. (hot describes the soup)
  • My grandmother is very kind. (kind describes my grandmother)

📌 Lesson 3 – Adjective Position
📍 Where Does the Adjective Go?
Adjectives usually go in two positions: before a noun, or after the verb "to be" (am, is, are).
Before a Noun
Adjective + Noun
"I have a big dog." · "She bought a blue bag."
After "to be"
Subject + am/is/are + Adjective
"The dog is big." · "Her bag is blue."
✨ Compare the Sentences
  • This is a delicious cake. → The cake is delicious.
  • I saw a scary movie. → The movie was scary.
  • He is a brave boy. → He is brave.

📌 Lesson 5 – Using Adjectives in Writing
✏️ Make Your Writing Colourful
Using adjectives helps readers imagine what you are describing. Compare these two sentences — which one is more interesting?
📄 Without vs. With Adjectives

❌ Plain: "I have a dog. It plays in the garden."

✅ With adjectives: "I have a small, fluffy dog. It plays happily in the green and colourful garden."

📄 Sample Paragraph – My Classroom

My classroom is big and bright. It has white walls and large windows. There are colourful posters on the wall. My desk is clean and tidy. My classmates are friendly and helpful. I feel happy when I am at school.

💡 Tips for Using Adjectives
🎯
Be Specific Instead of "good", try "delicious", "amazing", or "wonderful" to be more precise.
🔢
Don't Overdo It Use 1–2 adjectives per noun so your sentence stays clear and natural.
📍
Check the Position Remember: adjective before a noun, or after "is/am/are".
📚
Build Your Word Bank Learn new adjectives every day and try using them in your sentences.
🔄
Learn Opposites Together Learning a word and its opposite at the same time helps you remember both!
🗣️
Use Them When Speaking Practise using adjectives in conversation, not just in writing.
